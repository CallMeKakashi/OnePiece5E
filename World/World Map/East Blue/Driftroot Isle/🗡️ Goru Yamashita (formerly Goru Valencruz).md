Backstory

Goru Valencruz  was born into one of the lesser noble houses of the Silk Court—**House Velencruz**, a family with fading prestige but loud ambitions. His older brother, **Nyx**, was only two years older but always felt like a world apart. When they were young, they were inseparable—running through the courtyards with their dog **Mikey**, sword-fighting with sticks, laughing until their ribs ached. Nyx was everything Goru looked up to.

Their **grandfather, Lord Nero Velencruz**, a retired swordsman and once a noble of great renown, raised them with a sense of honor, pride, and tradition. He gifted Goru a ceremonial training sword on his seventh birthday. “You may be sickly,” he said, “but you carry the name Velencruz. Be regal. Be strong. Be elegant.”

But Goru’s father, **Laziel Velencruz**, was nothing like his own father. Laziel never earned power—he inherited it, manipulated it, and twisted it to serve his ambition. Where Nero believed in legacy through merit, Laziel believed in domination through fear. And he saw promise in Nyx. A boy he could mold.

One day, when Mikey fell gravely ill, their father promised he could “fix” him. He sent the dog to someone in the Silk Court. Mikey returned days later, altered. No longer the joyful creature they loved, but something colder… obedient… wrong. Nyx renamed him **Morrow**.

As Nyx grew older, he changed. The playful older brother was gone—replaced by a boy desperate for approval, hardened by expectation. He laughed with the other nobles when they mocked Goru for his frailty. Even joined in. The bond between them cracked.

But Driftroot had more to offer than cold palaces. One fateful day, when Goru was left behind during one of his father's business visits, he wandered into the **Old City**—terrified and lost. There, a girl named **Sana** found him crying near a bakery. She smiled, held out her hand, and asked if he wanted to play.

Through her, Goru met **Leon**, a fierce, impulsive boy who challenged adults twice his size and refused to bow to authority. They became fast friends. While Goru still returned to the Silk Court, he began sneaking out to train with Leon, ignoring his grandfather's insistence on “elegance” and embracing Leon’s raw, streetwise swordsmanship. It made him stronger. The bullying stopped.

But Laziel eventually found out.

He called them **“the poison.. It will be cleansed,” he said.

Goru didn’t understand what that meant until the fire.

When he was fifteen, the Old City erupted in flames. Orphanages, homes, shops—all swallowed by fire. People screamed, choked, and burned. The nobles watched. Said it was “tragic but unavoidable.”

But then a **Marine Commodore** —a visiting officer named **Horus**—arrived. He dove into the fire, saving as many as he could. Only **Sana and Leon** survived. Sana was gravely injured and in a coma for months. Leon never left her side—until the day he didn’t.

Thane offered to take Leon with him. Leon accepted. “She wouldn’t want me to stay,” he said to Goru. “She’d want me to become something more.” Then he was gone.

Years passed.

While helping prepare for **Nero’s funeral**, Goru stumbled upon an old document in Laziel’s chest. A plan, detailed and cruel: how to purge the “poison” of Driftroot through the **sewer systems** beneath the Old City. It wasn’t an accident. His father—and likely other nobles—had planned the fire.

That day, Goru Valencruz died. He shed the name, took his mother's instead and became **Goru Yamashita**. He vanished from the Silk Court, moving into a fishing village near the coast, close to Sana, who had since recovered. Only a few knew his real identity. Laziel never searched for him. Perhaps he was glad to be rid of his “weak” son.

Goru found peace by the sea—until he overheard some guards at the docks.

Nyx hadn’t shown up for morning assembly. He had left for **Greymire** in a hurry. Rumors said he was working with **bandits**.

He should hate him. He abandoned him. Let him me be mocked. Let their dog become a monster. Let the Old City burn. But he was his brother.

Despite everything, he was in danger. And Goru couldn’t ignore that.

He picked up his blade again. He would go to Greymire.

Let the past finally reckon with the present.
