# ⚓ **Prologue: The Birth of the Motley Crew**

### _"In a world ruled by tides and tyranny, fate drew together those who were never meant to meet."_

---

## **Year 1476, Month 1 – Port Orlin, North Blue**

### **Astor's First Voyage**

For the first time, **Astor**, a young man from Loguetown, sets sail into the wider world. His connections within the Marines have revealed whispers of an **illicit deal** happening on **Port Orlin**, a modest island in the North Blue. Seeking information and opportunity, Astor travels there—unaware that his path will cross with destiny itself.

---

### **Ronan and the Devil's Prize**

Elsewhere on the island, **Ronan**, a seasoned bounty hunter, waits for a buyer. During his last hunt, he intercepted a **Devil Fruit**—the very treasure the pirates he pursued sought to protect.  
Now, he hides it, uncertain whether to sell or safeguard it.

---

### **Pasha's Escape**

On that same night, under the silver gaze of the moon, **Pasha**, a lion mink, breaks free from a Germa 66 research facility. Subjected to cruel experiments under **Dr. Nikolai**, he finally lost control—transforming into his **Sulong form** and tearing through the walls of his prison. Bloodied and burned, he flees into the night, guided only by instinct and the faint hope of freedom.

---

### **The Tavern at Dawn**

As the sun rises, Astor sits by the docks, conversing with an old fisherman. The man warns him of **ships vanishing in the night**, swallowed near the warehouses—an omen of something dark lurking in Orlin's waters.  
Inside the nearby tavern, **Ronan** waits quietly. **Linus**, a man in a white coat, nurses a drink—haunted by guilt.

Then the door bursts open. **Pasha**, barely standing, enters, his orange prison jumpsuit torn and soaked in blood. Linus, recognizing Germa's handiwork, acts instinctively—dragging him to the pantry to tend his wounds. When the two return, Astor joins them at their table, feigning drunken curiosity.

Moments later, **bandits** burst through the door—mercenaries hired by Dr. Nikolai to retrieve his escaped "specimen." Gunfire erupts. Before a bullet can strike Pasha, Linus grabs the others and **phases through the floor**, taking them into the tavern's basement.

There, the four strangers make their stand—emerging victorious and discovering a crate of **Lunafang Serum**, a substance capable of granting temporary mink-like traits.

They realize they share a common enemy—and flee the island aboard the bandits' stolen ship.

---

## **The Black Market – Krake's Maw**

Their journey leads them to **Krake's Maw**, a pirate haven and black market where even Marines tread carefully. The island is alive with noise and vice; tonight, a grand contest of pirates is underway. The prize? The bounty of a crew the group themselves had slain back in Orlin—now falsely claimed by a man named **Grinjaw**, the event's host.

Ronan's pride will not allow it. He convinces the crew to participate.

After several intense rounds, they reach the finals—only for the celebration to shatter as **Commodore Horus**, a fearsome Marine commander, leads a raid on the island.  
Outmatched, the crew is overwhelmed. Just as defeat looms, the world blurs—time slows—and they awaken on their ship, adrift under the stars.

Two figures stand nearby: **Veyl** and **Serica Corven**.

- **Veyl**, a purple-haired man with a smirk and the **Speed Speed Fruit**, claims to have rescued them mid-battle.
    
- **Serica**, calm yet sharp-eyed, wields the **Weapon Weapon Fruit** and watches them like an experiment.
    

They belong to a mysterious organization called **Sixfold**, and their leader has requested to meet the crew on **Magnolia Island**, near Reverse Mountain.

---

## **Magnolia Island – The Sixfold Armada**

The journey is perilous. At sea, Veyl summons a **sea beast** through vibrations in the water, giving the crew their first true taste of grand-scale combat. Together, they slay the monster and earn a newfound respect for their strange allies.

Upon arrival, they behold **The Armada**—Sixfold's headquarters, a fortress built atop a chain of ships anchored together.  
Here they learn that the Sixfold communicate through enchanted **paper sigils** that serve as identification, trackers, and even messaging origami.

While awaiting the return of the organization's leader, the crew seeks a ship sturdy enough to cross the Grand Line. They meet **Bramble**, a gruff shipwright, who offers to build one if they can clear out the bandits plaguing his supply routes. The raiders are led by **Kirro**, a crab-fishman—and Ronan's old crewmate under **Captain Morley**, his late mentor.

Ronan pleads with Kirro to stop, but the fishman refuses. The fight ends in tragedy.

---

## **The Princess of Virellis**

Back in Magnolia's tavern, **The Slated Journey**, the crew witnesses Marines harassing a young woman. Astor intervenes, leading to a street brawl.  
The woman reveals herself as **Aralia Cindross**, princess of the **Kingdom of Virellis**, now usurped by her younger brother. Her elder brother leads a resistance but lacks the strength to reclaim the throne. She seeks allies—true warriors.

Moved by her plea, Astor convinces the others to help. Their decision will ripple through history.

---

## **The Return of Liz**

When Sixfold's leader returns, Linus's heart stops—it's his sister, **Liz**, long thought dead.  
She reveals that she survived the Germa facility by fully awakening her **Paper Paper Fruit**, reconstructing her own body from the pages of her research. For years, she wandered without memories, until the incident at Port Orlin awakened fragments of her past.

Their reunion is bittersweet. Liz confirms that **Vorro**, one of Sixfold's senior agents, was involved in the **pirate raid that destroyed Ronan's island**, and that his crew took several orphans—including Ronan's siblings—to **Virellis**. Vorro now serves under **Captain Briggs**, a Marine commanding the region's covert operations.

Determined to find his family, Ronan swears to follow Aralia's cause.

With their new ship complete, the group finally christens themselves:

### **The Motley Crew**

---

## **The Grand Line – Two Years Later**

Over the next two years, the Motley Crew carves their legend into the waves.

- They rescue **Cline the Plague**, a former convict from a Marine prison convoy bound for Impel Down.
    
- They uncover traces of **Project MIST**, a joint experiment by **Vegapunk**, **Judge**, and **Caesar Clown**, merging science and sorcery.
    
- They destroy production sites of **defective Lunafang Serum**, discovering that all derive from experiments on **Subject 0 – Pasha**.
    
- They reunite with **Blackhand Cane**, Astor's mentor, who soon vanishes again—rumored to possess a mysterious Devil Fruit.
    
- They encounter **Leon**, a Marine cadet trained by Horus—an honorable man caught in a corrupt system. Despite clashing, mutual respect forms between Leon and the crew.
    

Near the close of their second year, **Aralia gives birth** to **Luna**, her daughter with Astor, named after her grandmother.

Meanwhile, shadows tighten their grip on the world. The truth emerges: **Rias** and **Silas**, two Sixfold members, have betrayed the organization, working under **Vorro** and **Captain Briggs** to orchestrate the Virellis coup and control the Lunafang network.

In the ensuing battle, Ronan unlocks the secret of his **Fusion Fusion Fruit**, merging with Astor to form **Vireth**—a single being combining both their skills: Astor's swordsmanship and Ronan's Fishman Karate. Together, they strike down Rias and prepare to confront the storm gathering in the New World.

Their next destination:

### **Sabaody Archipelago — The Gateway to Destiny.**
