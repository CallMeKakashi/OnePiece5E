# Blackhand Cane — Raid Kingdom

Blackhand raids Raid Kingdom to recover Mira. Split from the composite Old Notes `Baptiste.md` import.

The deck of the _Queen Anne's Revenge_ creaked under the weight of a restless sea and a crew restless for answers. Blackhand Cane stood with his back to the rail, one boot planted on a coil of rope, the salt spray tasting like iron on his lips. He listened as the murmurs rose and fell like gulls over the water.

"Yer tellin' me she willingly let 'em take her?" Cane's voice was low and threaded with a slow-burning fury—equal parts disbelief and pride.

Tray Vexen, still wiping ale from his beard, spat on the planks and shrugged. "I tried to run with who I could, boss. She wouldn't budge. Told me to run to the old man and say she'd be back in a few weeks. When I got in, they had her chained—strange chains, boss. She couldn't move proper. Cannon fire got Thompson and the rest when we tried to come in." He tossed his mug to the side and clenched his fists; the tremor in his voice betrayed his bravery.

Renzo—first mate, helmsman, and a basking-shark fishman whose jaw could cleave a hull—leaned forward. "This ain't right, Cane. The Navy's footprint there is… heavier'n usual. This reaches higher than a local magistrate. If the Admiralty's pokin' 'round, we can't be drawin' that kind of heat."

Morley, an angelshark fishman with a map scarred across one cheek, rubbed his neck and growled, "We can't leave Mira there. Not like that."

Blackhand Cane let the words settle. He thought of Mira—sharp as a tack, strong fist than most men, and loyal in a way that made fools of better men.

"We can't afford casualties here," he said finally, and his crew heard more than a plan in that sentence—they heard restraint. Tray's shoulders sagged but then straightened again, the stubbornness of youth clashing with the fear under his ribs.

"I can't just leave her, Cap'n. If you don't authorize a raid I'll march there myself, even if it kills me. Sir!" Tray's shout cracked across the deck.

Cane stepped down, the grin that never quite reached his eyes softening as he rested a hand on Tray's shoulder. "She's got a good friend, that one," he rumbled. "But ye misunderstan' me, lad. When I said "minimize casualties," I meant not ours—it's not our blood I'm frettin' over. 'Tis theirs."

Tray blinked, confusion and loyalty warring in his face. Cane's hand tightened for a heartbeat, then loosened. "A fine heart ye've got. Good. We can't have one o' our best die for naught. We raid the island, we fetch Mira back, and any soul stands in our way—well, ye know what to do."

He unrolled the stolen layout that Tray had snatched up, and Cane's finger traced the ink as if tasting the plan. "We don't know exactly where they've put 'er. I'll split ye into teams. Morley, lead Goro and Droven—keep yer heads and don't go moon-feelin' brave."

Droven, snorted. Morley punched him lightly; the gruff affection made the others crack a grin even while the knot of worry grew.

"Renzo," Cane said, "you're with Tray. Find out what they hid and why the Navy's got eyes on this. If there's a ledger, a name, somethin' worth takin', bring it back. But remember—priority's Mira."

Gansai, the spider-crab shipwright with hands like pincers and a mind for rigging, lurked by the mizzen. "I'll hold the ship and the kids safe, Boss. The _Queen Anne's_ won't be takin' on no prisoners without a fight."

Cane's grin returned. "Good. I'll go raise a ruckus in the center—draw most o'their forces like bait to a trap. When I go, make sure the rest o' you strike quick and clean. Tray—you're the heart of this tit-for-tat. Keep her close, and bring her back."

Tray swallowed, eyes bright with unshed tears and the kind of courage that made fools legends. "Aye, Captain."

As the _Queen Anne's Revenge_ set a course toward the island, Cane watched his crew organize with that terrible calm captains learn. He felt the old pull in his chest—the promise he would not break. Steel or no steel, chain or no chain, they would reach into whatever belly of iron or law had swallowed Mira and drag her back.

He spat into the ocean, the salt and the promise mixing. "If they think ta take what's ours, they'll find the sea full o' teeth. Set sail. Raid Kingdom's got a storm comin', an' we're the thunder."
