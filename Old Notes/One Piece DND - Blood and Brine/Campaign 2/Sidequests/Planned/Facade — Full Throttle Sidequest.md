# Sidequest: **“Facade — Full Throttle”**

**Hook:** While sailing from Anchorpoint to Driftroot, Facade creaks; his autorepair protocols need concentrated capsaicin (a rare fuel). He detects a capsaicin source ~10 miles west: the spice island **Freefield**. The party lands to repair Facade — but the island’s chillies are being stolen/burned at night. Merchants hire the party for a night watch; what they find raises moral choices.

---

## Short summary (what happens in play)

1. Arrive at Freefield — bustling spice market, merchants selling chiles.
    
2. Meet Torik the Steelhand (travelling smith) who has a marine-grade cannister & hoses (can be bought/repaired).
    
3. Merchants ask for paid security; party stakes out the warehouses/fields that night.
    
4. Night raid: a crew is attempting to burn/ruin fields and steal chillies. They capture a leader who reveals the merchant smugglers brought spices and enslaved Alabastan people.
    
5. Party chooses: protect merchants (kill/capture raiders), help the raiders free slaves (rebel vs merchants), or find a middle way (expose merchants).
    
6. Possible follow-up: pirate crew (hired or otherwise) — three pirates called **The Brine Rats** show up as complication/backup.
    

---

## Locations & set dressing

- **Freefield Market:** Colorful stalls heavy with peppers; pungent pepper aroma; locals in woven hats; a few shady stalls.
    
- **Warehouse Row / Plantation:** Low stone warehouses, rows of drying racks, fields surrounded by low fog at night. Lanterns, small watch towers.
    
- **Torik’s Forge (travelling smith):** Portable anvil, several hoses & a marine spray canister labeled for “fire brigades.” Torik is honest and oblivious to the smuggling (he commissions marine canisters regularly).
    
- **Slave Pens (hidden):** Small, guarded pens behind a warehouse; barely enough room for people.
    

---

## Key NPCs

### Facade (player-character/ally)

- Needs: **Concentrated capsaicin** to trigger autorepair; also needs a **hose** and **marine cannister** to collect/store/use it.
    
- Mask broken — needs replacement hose to connect to cannister.
    

### Malak (player)

- Knows navigation, some diplomacy and probably language to recognize Alabastans.
    

### **Torik the Steelhand** — Travelling Smith

- Honest journeyman smith. Carries one marine-grade cannister & hoses (used for fire brigades).
    
- Price: 1500–3000 berries (negotiable / barter + help).
    
- Willing to sell/repair once paid; shocked if told merchants are slavers.
    

**Personality/Hook Lines**

- “I don’t ask where the job goes, I just fix the kit. Money pays, problem solved.”
    
- If pressed: genuinely naive about source of spice.
    

### **Merchant Consortium**

- Leaders of the plantations/warehouses. Will hire party for night watch for a small fee (2,000 berries total — pay regardless of outcome).
    
- Have men; prefer thieves killed — strongly imply “dead or gone.”
    

### **Ramel (Raider leader)** — Freedom Fighter (anti-merchant)

- Leader of the small raiding group that appears at night. Claims merchants smuggled spice and enslaved Alabastans; his goal is to free people and disrupt supply lines.
    
- He’s sympathetic: will tell truth if captured/interrogated.
    

### **The Brine Rats** — Pirate backup

- Name for the pirate trio hired/used by merchants or independent opportunists. They can appear if things escalate.
    

---

## Encounters & Mechanics

### Scene 1: Market — Buy/Investigate

- **Perception/Insight checks (DC 12–15):** Spot nervous laborers, odd travel tags on spice crates marked “Alabasta Transit,” or a pen hidden behind a lean-to.
    
- **Investigation/History (DC 14):** Recognize Alabastan script or speak the dialect (Malak can notice).
    

### Torik interaction

- **Persuasion DC 12** to bargain price 10–30% off.
    
- If party reveals Torik the merchants’ shady ties, Torik becomes distressed but sells/repairs anyway if paid.
    

---

### Night: Stakeout

- **Stealth checks (DC 12):** To take good positions near fields.
    
- **Listen/Perception (DC 10–14):** Hear rustling approach.
    

**Raiders appear:** 7–8 raiders + leader (Ramel).

- Goal: set small fires, poison drying racks, free a pen of slaves.
    

**Option A — Stop them quietly**

- Nonlethal takedown possible via stealth/ grapple/hold actions. If surprised, easier.
    

**Option B — Confront**

- Combat vs raiders and possibly Brine Rats if merchants hired them as backup.

## Stat blocks (simple & flexible — tune to your system)

**Raider (Grunt)** — _use ~1 HP per level party; adjust_

- HP: 10 (3d6 + 3)
    
- AC: 12 (leather + dodge)
    
- Attack: Short spear +4 to hit, 1d6+2 piercing
    
- Special: **Molotov Toss** (once per raider per raid) — throw a crude incendiary that deals 1d6 fire and can ignite dry rack (environmental hazard).
    

**Ramel (Leader)**

- HP: 30 (5d8 + 10)
    
- AC: 14 (leather + light chain)
    
- Attack: Scimitar +6, 1d8+3 slashing; dagger +5, 1d4+2
    
- Special Abilities: **Quick Parry:** +2 to AC vs first attack each round. **Freedom Strike:** When reduced below half HP, attempts to disengage & escape to free slaves (leaders use morale).
    

**Brine Rat Pirate (named mini-bosses)** — _three pirates of varied types_

- **Captain Harl (cutthroat)** HP 35 AC 15 Attack scimitar +6 1d8+4; has **Pistol (range)** 1d10 once per encounter.
    
- **Sable (swashbuckler)** HP 28 AC 14 Attack rapier +6 1d8+3; **Evasion** when dex save.
    
- **Bilge (bruiser)** HP 40 AC 13 Attack heavy cudgel +5 1d10+4; **Grapple** to pin.
    

_(If you run D&D 5e, the raiders ≈ CR 1/2, Ramel ≈ CR 2, Brine Rats combined ~CR 3–4. Scale numbers down for fewer PCs.)_

## Environment & hazards

- Dry racks and spice fields are highly flammable: fire spreads quickly (use 5-ft/10-ft rules for your system). A single Molotov can start a field fire (ticking hazard).
    
- Cannisters in Torik’s caravan — can be used as explosive barrels if hit (dangerous).
    

---

## Moral/Strategic Choices & Consequences

### 1) Help the Merchants (Protect crops / kill raiders)

- Outcome: Merchants pay the promised fee (2,000 berries). Torik sells cannister/hose for full price. Merchants reward with small tip (500 berries).
    
- Reputation: Party becomes known as muscle; merchants may later call upon them. However, the slaves remain captive — potential future guilt/plot hooks.
    
- If they kill raiders: locals may fear them; Ramel’s allies may retaliate later (Brine Rats attack later).
    

### 2) Aid the Raiders (Free slaves & sabotage merchants)

- Outcome: Free slaves; big moral win. Torik is upset but sells hoses if convinced or paid; merchants call it a crime — bounty posted (future complication).
    
- Loot: raiders/warehouses contain concentrated capsaicin stores (enough to repair Facade) + merchant ledgers revealing smuggling routes (future quest).
    
- The Brine Rats (if present) may fight or flee. Merchants may hire bounty hunters later.
    

### 3) Expose merchants (Capture evidence, spare blood)

- Outcome: Steal ledgers, rescue slaves quietly, and present proof to a neutral authority (if available) or a sympathetic magistrate on Driftroot / Anchorpoint. Torik helps if convinced.
    
- Reward: moderate pay from abolitionist groups; reputational benefit; merchants likely to hire assassins.
    

### 4) Neutral / Mercantile play

- Take the fee, arrest a few raiders nonlethally, hand them to local law (if law exists). Possible but less dramatic.

## Capsaicin & Facade Repair (mechanical suggestions)

- **Concentrated Capsaicin (consumable / fuel):** Each 1 unit in a **Marine Cannister** restores **X HP** or triggers one autorepair cycle that heals Facade for 1d8 + party level and removes one functional debuff (rusted limb = -1 to attack/speed). Reserve units needed: _3–5 units_ for full repair depending on severity.
    
- **Marine Cannister & Hose (item):** Necessary to store capsaicin. If Torik sells a marine cannister, it also has a mechanical benefit: when attached to Facade, Facade can use **Overdrive** once per long rest — +2 to all physical checks for 1 minute at cost of 2 capsaicin units and overheating afterwards (temporary -1 to actions until next long rest).
    
- **Repair Procedure:** Requires 30 minutes and a smith or tinkerer check (Tinker/Smith DC 12) to fit hose; if TORIK is present he does it automatically.
    

---

## Loot / Rewards

- **From merchants (payment):** 2,000–3,000 berries for the night watch (paid regardless). Tip 500 berries.
    
- **From warehouses / freed slaves:** 3–8 units of concentrated capsaicin (enough to repair Facade), crates of spices worth 1,500–6,000 berries (moral choice to sell or return).
    
- **From pirates:** pistols (one), minor spoil like maps, small trinkets.
    
- **XP / Leveling:** Treat raid as short quest — give XP equal to a medium encounter + roleplay bonus; freeing slaves and finding ledger give extra XP and campaign hooks.

## Dialogue Snippets (use in scene)

**Torik:** “You lot look like you fix problems. I fix metal. This here? For the island brigades. You need one? That’ll be 2,500 berries. Not my business where it goes. I’m a smith, not a judge.”

**Merchant Foreman (smug):** “We’re honest tradesmen. You’ll be paid to stand and keep watch. Handle thieves — dead or alive — and the pay is yours. No questions.”

**Ramel (if captured, bitter):** “You call them merchants, but I call them smugglers. Alabasta’s people were taken — fields burned where they refused. I set fire to their tags because I can’t walk past people in chains.”

**Facade (robotic, cracked voice):** “Warning: repair protocol offline. Capsaicin detected: 10 miles west. Please secure container for infusion. Efficiency low without hose.”

## Scenes & Beats — Playable Flow

1. **Arrival & Market (20–30 minutes)**
    
    - Roleplay, bargaining, clues if players investigate (DCs above).
        
2. **Night watch stakeout (15–30 minutes)**
    
    - Use stealth checks; let players pick positions.
        
3. **Raid & Confrontation (combat or stealth) (30–60 minutes)**
    
    - If nonlethal, allow capture and interrogation.
        
4. **Aftermath (15–30 minutes)**
    
    - Decision time; Torik sells/fits hose if paid; capsaicin obtained or not.
        
5. **Resolution & Hooks**
    
    - Merchants may place bounties or hire the Brine Rats again; ledgers point to Alabasta-smuggling route; freed slaves could join the crew or provide info.
        

---

## Complications / Optional twists

- Torik is in debt to the merchants; if players antagonize merchants, Torik may become pressured.
    
- The Brine Rats might be pigeonholed as independent pirates who were hired but then set to “cover up” the raid by burning fields.
    
- A local magistrate is corrupt: bringing evidence to them accomplishes nothing. Players must hide or take evidence to a larger port.
    

---

## Running with one player missing (GM notes)

- This quest naturally centers Facade & Malak—perfect for when one player is absent. If a second player is missing:
    
    - Scale down numbers: use 4–5 raiders instead of 7–8; Brine Rats can flee early.
        
    - Make Torik more helpful: he can do the mechanical repair semi-automatically and assist in a fight (fights are more dramatic if NPCs chip in).
        
    - Facade can have a temporary self-repair protocol that restores a small amount of function to keep things balanced (gives Facade an active contribution even with fewer PCs).
        
- If you still need to shorten runtime: skip the moral interrogation & cut to a single decisive scene (either a quick raid fight or a stealth rescue).

## Quick GM Cheat-sheet (one page)

- **Market checks:** Perception 12 → see hidden pens; Investigation 14 → Alabastan tags.
    
- **Torik price:** 2,500 berries for marine cannister + hose; repair fitting check DC 12 (takes 30 min).
    
- **Night foes:** 7 raiders (HP 10, AC 12), 1 Ramel (HP 30, AC 14). Optional: 3 Brine Rats (Captain, Sable, Bilge).
    
- **Capsaicin units:** 3–5 units needed to fully restore Facade; each unit = one crate in warehouses or carried by raiders.
    
- **Rewards:** 2–3k berries (merchant pay), 3–8 cap units, ledgers (plot hook).
    
- **Main choice results:** Protect merchants → paid, but moral consequences. Aid raiders → free slaves, bounty on party. Expose merchants → future allies, politics.

## Hooks for continuing campaign

- Ledger reveals smuggling routes to Alabasta → link to bigger arc (perfect tie to character backstories).
    
- Freed slaves know names, places, or a lost mentor (use to link Blackhand Cane disappearance or Germa experiments).
    
- Merchants place bounties, or the Brine Rats plot revenge → recurring villains.
