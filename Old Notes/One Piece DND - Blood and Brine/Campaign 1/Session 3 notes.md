
# **Quest: The Davy Back Gambit**

### **Part 1: The Call**

- **Trigger:** As the crew is preparing to leave for the supply run, a loud ringing echoes through the ship.
    
- The ringing **Den Den Mushi** is found behind a set of barrels, tucked away in the shadows of the lower deck.
    
- The snail has a twisted expression — **wide eyes and a sinister smirk**, reflecting the unsettling personality of its owner.

### **Part 2: The Voice**

- When answered, the voice on the other end speaks in a cold, Russian-accented tone:
    
    > "**Ah… So you are still alive, Subject-0. Or perhaps I should say... Pasha.**"
    
- The scientist mocks the crew’s efforts to hide, remarking that **Judge Vinsmoke** has eyes everywhere.
    
- He taunts Pasha, reminding him of the **countless experiments** performed in the name of Germa's ambition.
    
- The scientist implies that Pasha's escape was part of a **calculated plan**, though it’s unclear whether this is the truth or psychological manipulation.
    
- **Dialogue Choice:**
    
    - **Pasha can remain silent.** The scientist grows more condescending, insisting that fear is expected.
        
    - **Pasha can lash out.** The scientist chuckles, remarking on his “predictable” emotions, further attempting to agitate him.
        
    - **Linn or Astor can intervene.** The scientist may acknowledge Linn’s history with Germa, warning him that his past will catch up.
        
- **Key Revelations:**
    
    - Germa is developing further iterations of the **Lunafang Serum**.
        
    - **Judge** has personally taken an interest in Pasha’s escaped state.
        
    - The bounty hunters in **Port Orlin** were a **distraction** — Germa has more dangerous forces in pursuit.
        
    - A hint that **"Subject-0"** was not the only project of its kind.
        

---

### **Part 3: The Threat**

- The call ends with the scientist's chilling words:
    
    > "**You cannot outrun the shadow of Germa, Subject-0. When Judge decides it is time, he will reclaim what is his. Enjoy your freedom while it lasts.**"
    
- The Den Den Mushi abruptly disconnects. The lingering tension leaves Pasha shaken, memories from his past resurfacing.
    
- Linn and Astor may attempt to reassure him, but the looming threat of Germa 66 is clear.
    

---

### **Aftermath**

- The trio now has a sense of **urgency**. While the supply run remains the priority, they are aware that they are **being watched**.
    
- The crew may choose to investigate the origin of the call or remain low-profile.
    
- Pasha is faced with a choice — to continue running, or to confront the ghosts of his past.
    
- Ronan remains unaware of the call unless the crew chooses to share it.

---

### **Part 4: Arrival at Krake's Maw**

- The crew disembarks at **Krake's Maw**, a twisted, lawless island known for smuggling and underground dealings.
    
- A filthy young boy offers them a fake ticket, claiming it’s needed to stay on the island without trouble.
    
- The boy scurries away after payment, laughing at their gullibility.
    

---

### **Part 5: The Vanishing Ship**

- After purchasing supplies in the chaotic black market, the crew returns to find their ship **missing**.
    
- Panic sets in as they search the empty dock.
    
- A greasy, weathered shipwright named **Bailen** approaches them.
    
    - Bailen sneers and says, “Shouldn’t have trusted anyone here. This is **Krake’s Maw**. No ship stays unclaimed for long.”
        
- He demands **10,000 Berries** for information.
    
- Once paid, Bailen reveals that the infamous **Hookfang Crew** took their ship as part of their ongoing crew competition.
    
- He warns that Hookfang's crew is dangerous and that their only way to reclaim the ship is through the competition.
    

---

### **Part 6: The Hookfang Crew’s Challenge**

- The crew is directed to the docks where the massive, ragged ship of the **Hookfang Crew** looms.
    
- There, they meet the boisterous captain, **Grinjaw**, who immediately recognizes them.
    
    - Grinjaw: “Are these the lads who beat Iron Grip in Port Orlin? A **motley** group of fellas you are!”
        
- Grinjaw invites them to participate in the **Davy Back Fight**, offering a twisted grin.
    
    - If they **win**, they’ll receive **250,000 Berries**—enough to buy a new ship from Bailen.
        
    - If they **lose**, they must give up something of value.
        
- The crew must appoint a **captain** and decide on a crew name.
    
    - **Linn** sarcastically suggests “The Motley Crew.”
        
- The captain must wager something valuable as a sign of confidence—it could be a weapon, gold, or even a personal item.
    

---

### **Part 7: The Games**

The games will take place on Hookfang's massive ship.

1. **Barrel Throw**
    
    - A strength-based challenge.
        
    - Contestants throw heavy barrels across the deck, aiming for distance.
        
    - Opponents include massive pirates with brute strength.
        
    - Success requires a combined Strength check from participating crew members.
        
2. **Plank Hop**
    
    - A balance and agility challenge.
        
    - Participants must run across a series of thin, unstable planks suspended over the ocean.
        
    - Dexterity checks are needed to maintain balance.
        
3. **Capture the Flag**
    
    - A team-based challenge.
        
    - The crew must retrieve a flag from the opposing side of the ship while defending their own.
        
    - Skill checks may involve Strength, Dexterity, or Intelligence depending on the tactics used.
        

---

### **Part 8: Outcomes**

#### **If the Crew Wins**

- Grinjaw begrudgingly hands over **250,000 Berries**.
    
- Bailen, though impressed, still demands full payment for a ship, but they’ll now have enough to buy one.
    
- Additionally, as a special reward, **Astor** receives the legendary **Shotgun Axe**, a weapon known for its brutal power.
    

#### **If the Crew Loses**

- Grinjaw demands the captain’s wager.
    
- Mockery and laughter echo across the ship as the crew is humiliated.
    
- However, the crew may choose to **challenge Grinjaw to a direct fight** to reclaim their ship and dignity.
    

---

### **Epilogue**

- Whether victorious or defeated, the crew's reputation grows.
    
- The Hookfang Crew now has their eyes on them.
    
- Meanwhile, whispers of **Red Fangs** and his defeat in Port Orlin spread further, raising concerns of who might come after them next.

Just before Linn, Pasha, and Astor leave the ship for a supply run, a **Den Den Mushi** rings from somewhere within the underbelly of the ship. When they find it, they are met with an unsettling voice — the chilling, heavily-accented tone of the **Germa Scientist** (Dr. Nikolai Vasiliev) who oversaw Pasha's experiments.

He attempts to manipulate Pasha, warning him that **Judge Vinsmoke** is displeased with his escape and that **consequences** are inevitable. The conversation reveals unsettling truths about Germa’s intentions and may even hint at **Pasha's true origins**.

### **Round 1: Barrel Ball**

- **Objective:** Knock opponents down by throwing barrels.
    
- **Mechanics:**
    
    - Each player rolls **Athletics** or **Strength** to throw a barrel.
        
    - Opponents roll **Dexterity** to dodge.
        
    - On a fail, they get knocked prone.
        
    - First team to knock down **three opponents** wins.
        

---

### **Round 2: Deckside Derby**

- **Objective:** Cross the wobbling deck planks without falling.
    
- **Mechanics:**
    
    - Players roll **Acrobatics** or **Dexterity** to keep their balance.
        
    - After every round, roll a **Perception** check to spot incoming obstacles like fish or water buckets.
        
    - On a fail, they slip and fall into the water, losing a point for the team.
        
    - First team with **three successful crossings** wins.
        

---

### **Round 3: Jolly Roger Jamboree**

- **Objective:** Steal the other crew's flag and return it to your side.
    
- **Mechanics:**
    
    - Players roll **Stealth** to sneak through the ship.
        
    - Guards use **Perception** to spot them.
        
    - If caught, players can roll **Deception** or **Persuasion** to bluff their way out.
        
    - The first team to successfully return with the flag wins.
