## Scene 1: The Training

Ronan's fists pounded against the rough bark of a tree, his knuckles already raw and bleeding. Each punch sent a jolt of pain through his hands, but he grit his teeth and kept going.

> **Ronan (muttering)**: "Stupid old man. Asking me to do dumb things just because I asked him to teach me Fishman Karate."

The relentless training was Morley's idea. The old Fishman believed strength was not only in the body but also in enduring pain. Ronan, though frustrated, endured it — for he wanted to grow stronger.

Suddenly, the distant laughter of children turned to frantic shouting. A group of orphans from the island's small orphanage sprinted past him, panic etched on their faces. The eldest boy shouted toward the church, his voice cracking with fear.

> **Boy**: "Sister Elira! Sister Elira! The Marines… they’re demanding penance!"

## Scene 2: The Marine Confrontation

Ronan's heart pounded. He raced after them, pushing past villagers who avoided the commotion. When he arrived at the town square, a sickening sight awaited him.

A young girl named **Lina**, the youngest of the orphans, was on the ground, tears streaming down her face. Towering above her were several Marines, their pristine uniforms stark against the dirt-covered street. At the center sat **Captain Alston Briggs**, a smug grin plastered on his face. His boots rested on a crate like a makeshift throne.

Ronan's blood boiled. Without thinking, he grabbed a rock and hurled it straight at the Captain. But before it could reach its mark, a blur flashed in front of him. **Lieutenant Calder Voss**, the Captain's right hand, snatched the rock mid-air with an unsettling ease.

> **Captain Briggs (mocking)**: "Insolence! Is this how you treat your saviors, the Marines?"

He drew his pistol, the polished metal gleaming under the sun. He lazily aimed it at Ronan, amusement flickering in his eyes.

> **Captain Briggs**: "Perhaps I should teach you some respect."

## Scene 3: Morley's Sacrifice

Before the Captain could pull the trigger, a deep voice interrupted him.

> **Morley**: "Let it go."

Morley, the old Fishman, stepped forward, his presence commanding. Though his body was scarred and aged, there was a defiance in his eyes. Briggs chuckled darkly.

> **Captain Briggs**: "Fine. I'll let it go… if you stand for fifteen minutes while my men beat you."

The villagers watched in stunned silence as Morley nodded.

> **Morley**: "If that’s what it takes."

The Marines grinned, relishing the opportunity. They struck him relentlessly, fists pounding into his scaled skin. But Morley didn’t fall. Blow after blow, he stood firm. Blood trickled from his mouth, but the fire in his eyes never wavered.

When the fifteen minutes were up, Captain Briggs sneered.

> **Captain Briggs**: "Time’s up. You can go now."

But as Morley turned, the Captain’s smile twisted into something darker. He pulled a shotgun from his side.

> **Captain Briggs**: "Actually, I think I’ll teach you both a lesson."

A deafening blast echoed through the square. But Morley moved faster than Ronan could react, shielding him. The pellets tore into Morley’s back. He stumbled, blood seeping through his shirt.

> **Morley**: "I told you… you need to go."

Ronan's scream was lost in the chaos. Before Morley could retaliate, Lieutenant Voss struck. His fist pierced straight through Morley’s chest, the brutal sound chilling the air. The Captain laughed.

> **Captain Briggs**: "Filthy Fishman blood."

He turned to the cowering villagers.

> **Captain Briggs**: "This island is under Marine protection. I expect my regular protection fee. Fail to pay, and next time, I won’t be so lenient."

With that, the Marines boarded their ship and sailed away, leaving behind the broken remnants of the town's spirit.

## Scene 4: The Departure

Days passed, but the weight of the Captain's words remained. Ronan, haunted by the memory of Morley's sacrifice, resolved to free his people from the grip of the Marines.

He searched tirelessly, scouring the island for anything valuable. Through determination and luck, he stumbled upon a hidden cavern. Among the rusted chests and forgotten relics, one treasure stood out—a **Devil Fruit**.

> **Ronan**: "With this… I’ll finally have the power to fight back."

But his mission was not solely for revenge. He vowed to gather enough wealth to pay the Marines' protection fee, ensuring the safety of the orphanage and Sister Elira. Then, he would set sail for Fishman Island, to find Morley's family—his wife and daughter—and deliver the sorrowful news.

With the Devil Fruit secured, Ronan stepped aboard a small ship, the wind filling its sails.

> **Ronan**: "No more running. It’s time to fight."

### **Character Goals**

- **Short-Term Goal:** Gather enough money to escape the island with all the children and Sister Elira, freeing them from Marine extortion.
    
- **Long-Term Goal:** Travel to Fishman Island to meet Morley’s family and apologize for not being able to save him and tell them about the life he led when he left for the seas.
