
You take a nap while traveling to a position where you can stay close enough to intercept the vessel, the steady rhythm of the journey lulling you into sleep. You fall into a dream.

You are looking through your eyes.

Below you, the world stretches out, small and fragile. Cities crumble like they were never meant to last, mountains split open, and the oceans themselves seem to rise and reach upward, as if answering a call.

Ahead of you, through thick smoke and rising flames, figures begin to form.

Five of them.

At the center stands a man clad in armor of gold and sapphire, radiant without shining, his sword driven into the ground before him. His hand rests calmly on its hilt, and set within it is a stone where tiny grains of golden dust swirl endlessly, like a captured star.

Beside him stands a woman, still and composed, a closed third eye upon her forehead, and even without opening it, you feel as though she sees straight through everything you are.

To his left, a man descends from above, landing with unnatural control, black wings folding behind him. His hair is white, his eyes red pierce through your hide.
Further left stands a towering mink with the mane of a lion, his body marked with ancient tattoos. 
And on the other side of the woman stands a fishman of the deep, her form broad and unmoving, her presence heavy like the crushing depths of the ocean, patient and absolute.

They are small compared to what you are.

Insignificant.

And yet… something feels wrong.

For the first time, there is resistance. Not in them—but in you.

The man at the center tightens his grip on the sword’s hilt, and the golden dust within the stone begins to spiral faster, like something on the verge of collapse. Then he speaks, and his voice _reaches_ you with these simple words.

“This is the end for you, Aegir.”

And in that moment, you feel it—much like the death and destruction you have ensued on this world.. the true meaning of death.

You wake up.
