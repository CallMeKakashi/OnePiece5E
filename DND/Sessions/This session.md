# Session Outline — Dual Perspective Structure

## Opening Structure

The session constantly cuts between two perspectives:

1. **Roma’s Perspective** — isolated, psychological, survival-focused.
    
2. **The Party’s Perspective** — investigation, dread, preparation, and eventual collision.
    

The pacing should feel like both stories are racing toward the same inevitable catastrophe.

---

# PART I — ROMA’S PERSPECTIVE

## Scene 1 — The Island of Kara

The session opens quietly.

Roma awakens inside a monastery deep within the mountains of Kara.  
Cold winds pass through old wooden halls lined with prayer bells and hanging lanterns.  
The entire monastery is hidden among cliffs and pine forests above the clouds.

He remembers fragments:

- The drug.
    
- The transformation.
    
- Blood.
    
- Screaming.
    
- Loss of control.
    

At first everything seems peaceful.  
Monks move calmly through the halls.  
Meals are served.  
No one speaks much.  
The atmosphere feels spiritual… but wrong.

Roma notices:

- Doors locking at night.
    
- Heavy reinforced chains hidden beneath floorboards.
    
- Claw marks on ancient stone.
    
- Broken prayer statues.
    
- Blood stains scrubbed repeatedly.
    

The monastery is not healing Sulong.  
It is containing it.

---

## Scene 2 — The Head Monk

Roma meets the elder monk who oversees the monastery.  
A calm old mink monk whose body carries scars from Sulong transformations long ago.

The monk explains:

> “Sulong is not power.  
> Sulong is truth.  
> The moon strips away the mask.  
> Rage becomes hunger.  
> Fear becomes instinct.  
> Violence becomes freedom.”

Roma is told that his transformation was forced unnaturally.  
The drug shattered the natural balance.  
His Sulong no longer waits for the moon.  
It now exists inside him constantly.

The monk explains three stages:

1. The Beast.
    
2. The Hunger.
    
3. The Silence.
    

Most die in the second stage.

---

## Scene 3 — Training Begins

Roma undergoes brutal spiritual and physical training.

### Training Trials

#### Trial 1 — Meditation Under Moonlight

Roma must meditate while chained beneath reflected moonlight.  
He hears whispers.  
He sees hallucinations.  
The Sulong speaks using the voices of people he killed.

#### Trial 2 — Starvation Trial

He is denied food for days.  
The monks intentionally provoke predatory instincts.  
Raw meat is placed nearby.  
He must resist.

#### Trial 3 — Combat Restraint

Roma spars against monks while forbidden from attacking lethally.  
Every loss of control results in punishment.

#### Trial 4 — Isolation Cave

Roma is sealed inside a dark cave overnight.  
No sound.  
No light.  
Only his own heartbeat.  
By morning, the cave walls are torn apart.  
He doesn’t remember doing it.

---

## Scene 4 — The First Breakdown

During evening prayer, Roma suddenly smells blood from a monk with a small injury.  
The Sulong violently surges forward.

Describe:

- Dilated pupils.
    
- White fur rising.
    
- Teeth sharpening.
    
- Breathing becoming animalistic.
    
- Sound distortion.
    

Roma nearly attacks.  
Several monks immediately restrain him with seastone chains.  
The head monk watches silently.

No anger.  
No fear.  
Only disappointment.

Afterward the monk tells him:

> “You still believe the beast is separate from you.  
> That belief will kill everyone around you.”

---

## Scene 5 — The Revelation

The monastery reveals its true purpose.

Every monk here once lost control.  
Some massacred villages.  
Some killed their own crews.  
Some devoured people.

The monastery is where broken minks are sent when they become dangerous.  
Not all leave.

Roma discovers underground chambers:

- Massive claw marks.
    
- Reinforced cells.
    
- Prayer scripts written repeatedly on walls.
    
- Names carved into stone.
    
- Old execution weapons.
    

One chamber remains sealed.  
Inside is something still alive.

---

## Scene 6 — The Final Trial

The monks bring Roma to a cliff overlooking the full moon.

He is told:

> “Tonight you either master the beast… or become it forever.”

He is deliberately forced into Sulong.

The transformation is worse than before.  
The drug reacts violently.  
The sky seems distorted.  
He loses pieces of reality.

The head monk does not fight him.  
Instead he forces Roma to confront memories:

- Every person he killed.
    
- Moments he enjoyed violence.
    
- Times he wanted to lose control.
    

The truth becomes unavoidable:

Roma’s rage was never only caused by Sulong.  
The beast simply removed restraint.

The scene should end ambiguously.

Either:

- Roma finally gains partial control.
    
- Or the audience THINKS he has control.
    

Cut away before certainty is revealed.

---

# PART II — THE PARTY’S PERSPECTIVE

## Scene 1 — Roma Vanished

The party begins on another island after the chaos of Roma’s forced transformation.  
Roma is missing.  
No one knows where he was taken.

Rumors spread:

- A white monster destroyed parts of the island.
    
- Entire groups vanished.
    
- Marines are hunting a “moon devil.”
    
- Underground groups are searching for Sulong-related drugs.
    

The crew must investigate.

---

## Scene 2 — Following the Trail

The party tracks clues across the island.

Possible discoveries:

- Dead smugglers carrying experimental moon drugs.
    
- Cipher Pol involvement.
    
- Black market mink trafficking.
    
- A hidden ledger referencing “Project Lunar Fang.”
    
- References to Kara Monastery.
    

This section should feel investigative and paranoid.  
Someone powerful orchestrated Roma’s forced transformation.

---

## Scene 3 — The Moral Divide

The crew begins debating Roma himself.

Questions emerge:

- Was Roma always unstable?
    
- How much damage did he actually cause?
    
- Can he even be saved?
    
- Is Sulong turning him into a monster permanently?
    

Different party members should have radically different reactions.

This is important:

Roma is not physically present, but emotionally dominates every conversation.

---

## Scene 4 — Ambush at Sea

While sailing toward Kara, the crew is attacked.

Possible attackers:

- Moon-drug traffickers.
    
- Cipher Pol agents.
    
- Beast hunters.
    
- Former victims of Sulong incidents.
    

The attackers reveal more disturbing truths:

- Roma’s transformation was intentional.
    
- Someone wanted to weaponize Sulong.
    
- Other failed experiments exist.
    

The battle should feel desperate and chaotic.

---

## Scene 5 — Arrival at Kara

The island itself feels unnatural.

Describe:

- Endless fog.
    
- Mountain cliffs.
    
- Temple bells echoing through valleys.
    
- Strange silence.
    
- Large claw marks in trees.
    
- Animals avoiding certain areas.
    

Villagers refuse to discuss the monastery.  
Many seem terrified.

Some say:

> “The mountain eats monsters.”

Others say:

> “Sometimes the monsters come back down.”

---

## Scene 6 — The Ascent

The crew climbs toward the monastery.

Along the path they encounter:

- Broken prayer gates.
    
- Old monk graves.
    
- Massive gouges in stone.
    
- Chains hanging from trees.
    
- Bones.
    

Eventually they find signs of Roma.

Fresh claw marks.  
Destroyed sections of the mountain.  
Blood.

Something recently escaped containment.

---

# PART III — COLLISION

## Scene 1 — The Monastery in Ruins

The party reaches the monastery during total chaos.

Possible imagery:

- Buildings burning.
    
- Prayer bells ringing violently.
    
- Monks evacuating wounded.
    
- Snow stained with blood.
    
- Giant claw marks across entire structures.
    

The head monk is heavily wounded.

He tells the crew:

> “He was never fighting the beast.  
> He was fighting himself.”

Then:

> “Do not approach him unless you are prepared to kill him.”

---

## Scene 2 — Finding Roma

The party discovers Roma somewhere isolated:

Possible locations:

- A moonlit cliff.
    
- Deep forest.
    
- Ancient shrine.
    
- Frozen lake.
    

Roma is transformed.  
But different.

Not mindless.  
Not fully sane either.

His Sulong form now feels eerily calm.  
Predatory.  
Controlled.

The tension should come from uncertainty.  
Nobody knows if he is stable.  
Including Roma.

---

## Scene 3 — Final Emotional Confrontation

This is not immediately a combat scene.  
It is a roleplay-heavy confrontation.

Roma confronts the crew.  
The crew confronts Roma.

Topics include:

- Fear.
    
- Responsibility.
    
- Violence.
    
- Whether he enjoys killing.
    
- Whether the crew still trusts him.
    
- Whether he trusts himself.
    

At any moment this can explode into combat.

---

## Possible Endings

### Ending A — Controlled Acceptance

Roma gains partial mastery.  
He accepts the beast as part of himself instead of denying it.  
The crew remains uneasy but hopeful.

### Ending B — Catastrophic Relapse

Something triggers Roma.  
The session ends with a violent Sulong rampage.

### Ending C — Self-Imposed Exile

Roma leaves voluntarily.  
He believes he is too dangerous to remain with the crew.

### Ending D — Party Intervention

The crew physically restrains or defeats Roma to save him.  
The relationship changes permanently afterward.

---

# Tone & Themes

Core themes of the session:

- Rage versus identity.
    
- Whether violence is inherent or chosen.
    
- Fear of becoming a monster.
    
- Self-control versus repression.
    
- Brotherhood under pressure.
    
- Trauma and accountability.
    

The session should feel:

- Psychological.
    
- Tragic.
    
- Atmospheric.
    
- Emotionally exhausting.
    
- Tense even outside combat.
    

Combat should never feel like the true danger.  
The real danger is whether Roma can still remain himself.
