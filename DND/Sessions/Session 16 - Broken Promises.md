
# BROADCAST TOWER — FIRST FLOOR

### _Encounter: Synth of the Feedback_

**Recommended Level:** Party-level appropriate  
**Encounter Type:** Boss Delay / Narrative Gate  
**Objective:** Delay the party while key NPCs ascend the tower  
**Outcome:** Scripted defeat; party advances regardless of combat result

---

## AREA OVERVIEW

The first floor of the Broadcast Tower is a wide circular chamber of steel and glass. The room is dominated by:

- A **central open floor** with hanging metallic jingles suspended from the ceiling
    
- A **spiral staircase** in the northeast corner leading upward
    
- A **reinforced blast door** sealing the floor from outside
    
- Thick residual **smoke and heat distortion**, remnants of recent sonic discharge
    

Sound behaves unnaturally here—echoes linger longer than they should, and even footsteps carry a faint harmonic hum.

---

## READ-ALOUD TEXT (BOXED)

> The blast door shudders as its locks disengage.
> 
> With a violent hiss, it begins to open—and a scorching gust of air and smoke explodes outward, whipping cloaks and stinging eyes.
> 
> A shrill burst of feedback screams through the chamber before cutting off abruptly.
> 
> Inside, through the haze, you see a man seated at the center of the floor on a slowly turning swivel chair. A music keyboard is strapped to his arm, wires coiling up his sleeve. He presses a key—
> 
> **WHRRRR—KREEEE.**
> 
> Painful feedback rattles your skull.
> 
> The man adjusts his purple-tinted glasses, then lowers them just enough to look directly at Riff.
> 
> “Well… I wasn’t mistaken after all.”
> 
> “If it isn’t my dear, sweet cousin.”

---

## NPC: SYNTH

Synth is calm, theatrical, and cruel. He speaks as though he has already won.

### Initial Dialogue

> “Looks like you finally grew up. Decided to be useful for something—  
> even if it’s a futile attempt to restore the stupid dreams of the mutes.”
> 
> “Now then…”  
> “Which one of you am I supposed to take down?”

---

## SCRIPTED PARTY SPLIT

At this moment, **Riff steps forward**.

### Riff’s Declaration

> “Baptiste. I’ll take care of this.”
> 
> “You should get to the top before the Emperor wakes.”

Allow a brief pause for roleplay. Afterward, Baptiste may respond.

### Synth’s Reaction (as the party moves)

> “Run along.”  
> “I’ll be sure you all die soon enough.”

---

## ENVIRONMENTAL TRIGGER: CEILING JINGLES

As the party begins ascending the spiral staircase:

> The metallic jingles hanging from the ceiling begin to rotate—slowly at first.
> 
> Then faster.
> 
> Their edges sharpen, humming with lethal resonance.

**Tigor notices first.** Riff does not.

### Intervention

Tigor leaps forward, shoving Riff out of the way as the jingles snap downward like blades.

Both crash across the floor.

### Tigor’s Choice

Tigor turns to Baptiste:

> “Captain… I’d like to stay behind.”
> 
> “For some reason, I _hate_ this guy’s guts.”

Baptiste nods.  
The rest of the crew ascends.

**The blast door seals shut behind them.**

---

## ENCOUNTER BEGINS

### Synth vs. Riff & Tigor

This fight is **not meant to be won through normal combat**.

### Combat Rules (DM Notes)

- Synth **speaks every round**. Silence is not permitted.
    
- Each round, Synth chooses one personal topic to weaponize:
    
    - Riff’s rejection from Sound Five
        
    - Cadence
        
    - Riff’s family
        
    - The Emperor’s “choice”
        
- Environmental effects (sonic bursts, vibrating floor, rotating jingles) escalate as the fight progresses.
    
- Synth cannot be reduced to 0 HP through standard means.
    

---

## SYNTH’S TAUNT PROGRESSION

Use one per round or adapt freely.

**Round 1 — Inferiority**

> “Do you know why the Emperor didn’t choose you?”  
> “Too weak. Too quiet.”

**Round 2 — Cadence**

> “Cadence believed in you.”  
> “That was her mistake.”

**Round 3 — Family**

> “Your family clung to dreams.”  
> “Dreamers become echoes.”

**Round 4 — Sound Five**

> “Sound Five didn’t need you.”  
> “They needed monsters.”

**Final Round — Desperation**

> “I was chosen.”  
> “You were spared.”

---

## SCRIPTED CINEMATIC: SYNTH’S FALL

When the fight reaches its narrative peak:

> The feedback spirals out of control.
> 
> The jingles tear loose, spinning wildly as the entire floor begins to resonate.
> 
> Synth laughs—too hard, too loud.
> 
> “You still don’t get it, cousin.”

He slams both hands onto the keyboard.

> A deafening sonic implosion erupts outward.
> 
> White noise floods your senses.
> 
> Pain.
> 
> Silence.

Riff and Tigor are thrown violently in opposite directions, crashing into the edges of the chamber.

The last thing they see is Synth’s grin—

Before the floor beneath him collapses inward and the sound dies completely.

---

## AFTERMATH

- **Synth is defeated** (dead, missing, or buried—DM’s choice)
    
- **Riff and Tigor are unconscious**
    
- The first floor is silent once more
    

Above them, deep within the tower—

**The Emperor stirs.**

---

## TRAITS

### **Feedback Conductor (Softened)**

At the start of Synth’s turn, creatures within **10 ft** take  
**3 (1d6) thunder damage**.

> _Low but constant pressure without spike damage._

---

### **Chosen by Sound**

Synth cannot be reduced below **5 HP** by damage.

He is defeated only by the **Narrative Trigger** (_Sonic Implosion_).

---

### **Weaponized Mockery**

At the start of each of Synth’s turns, he targets **one creature he can see**.

That creature must succeed on a **DC 14 Wisdom saving throw** or suffer **one** effect (Synth chooses):

- Disadvantage on their next attack or spell
    
- Speed reduced to 0 until the start of their next turn
    
- **5 (1d10) psychic damage**
    

> _This replaces high damage with control + flavor._

---

## ACTIONS

### **Multiattack**

Synth makes **one Harmonic Strike** and may use **Mocking Chord** if available.

---

### **Harmonic Strike**

_Melee or Ranged Spell Attack:_ +6 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 40 ft., one target.  
**Hit:** **10 (2d6 + 3) thunder damage**

If the target is **Riff**, Synth may immediately insult him (no mechanical effect, roleplay only).

---

### **Mocking Chord (Recharge 4–6)**

Synth strikes a discordant note aimed at one creature within 30 ft.

The target must make a **DC 14 Constitution saving throw**:

- **Fail:** 14 (4d6) thunder damage and disadvantage on their next saving throw
    
- **Success:** Half damage, no disadvantage
    

---

### **Ceiling Jingle Snap (1/Encounter)**

Synth animates the hanging jingles.

One creature must succeed on a **DC 13 Dexterity saving throw** or take  
**11 (2d10) slashing damage**.

> _This is the “oh shit” moment — use once._

---

## REACTIONS

### **Resonant Flinch (1/Round)**

When Synth takes damage, he may reduce that damage by **5**.

> _Prevents burst killing without feeling unfair._

---

## LEGENDARY ACTIONS (LIMITED)

Synth has **1 Legendary Action per round**.

- **Discordant Whisper:** One creature within 30 ft takes **4 (1d8) thunder damage**
    
- **Shift Frequency:** Synth moves up to 15 ft without provoking opportunity attacks
    

---

## ENVIRONMENTAL PRESSURE (OPTIONAL)

On **Round 3**, introduce one effect:

- Feedback hum — disadvantage on Concentration checks this round
    

Do **not** repeat unless the fight drags.

---

## FINALE: SONIC IMPLOSION (SCRIPTED)

Trigger this when:

- Synth reaches **5 HP**, or
    
- You feel the emotional beats are complete (≈ 4–5 rounds)
    

**Effect:**

- A massive feedback surge detonates
    
- Riff and Tigor are reduced to **0 HP but stable**
    
- Synth is defeated (dead, buried, or vanished)
    
- The floor partially collapses
    

This **cannot be countered**.
