# Session Outline – “The Light Beyond the Horizon”

## Opening Scene — Tremors in the Night

The session begins deep into the night aboard the ship.

The sea is unnaturally calm at first. Then—

A low tremor.

Not violent enough to throw anyone off balance, but enough to rattle cups, shift hanging lanterns, and wake light sleepers.

The ship creaks.

Water ripples strangely.

### Baptiste’s Moment

Baptiste is the first to properly notice something is wrong.

When he steps onto deck, the horizon is dark… until suddenly—

A massive sphere of light blooms far beyond the sea line.

Not lightning.  
Not fire.

Something else.

The light rapidly expands, swallowing the horizon and painting the entire night sky white-gold for several seconds. The ocean reflects it like a second sun.

For a brief moment:

- shadows vanish,
- the stars disappear,
- the sea itself looks frozen.

Then the glow slowly fades.

Leaving only darkness again.

And silence.

An unnatural silence.

### Optional Party RP

If players wake up:

- they can gather on deck,
- speculate,
- argue,
- sense danger,
- or attempt checks.

Possible observations:

- the tremor came _after_ the light appeared,
- sea creatures below seem disturbed,
- compasses may twitch briefly,
- anyone spiritually perceptive may feel overwhelming pressure for a moment.

Do **not** explain the event yet.

Let it linger.

## Mira Arrives

Mira boards the ship casually but with noticeable urgency.

Julee is sitting on her shoulders half-asleep while eating something.

She tells everyone:

> “Training grounds. As soon as you can.”

Before leaving again, she updates them:

- Tom has decided to stay at Mira’s base temporarily,
- mostly to look after Crunch and Lou while things settle down.

As she walks off the ship, she tosses a newspaper onto the deck.

### Newspaper Hook

Headline implication:  
The crew caused major chaos recently.

Potential tone:

- exaggerated reporting,
- inflated damages,
- bounty rumors,
- witnesses contradicting each other,
- fear around the Lunarfold Pirates growing.

Mira’s only comment:

> “Well… you made quite a ruckus there.”

# Training Grounds Scene

The crew arrives at Mira’s training grounds.

This scene is primarily:

- tactical development,
- crew growth,
- foreshadowing,
- leadership pressure.

Mira gathers everyone before sparring begins.

---

## Mira’s Speech

Key points she should naturally cover:

### Team Coordination

- Individual strength is good.
- Survival through raw ability works… until it doesn’t.

### Battlefield Awareness

- A fight isn’t just about _your_ opponent.
- Positioning matters.
- Ally awareness matters.

### Example Lesson

Holding down a dangerous enemy sounds smart—  
but if that enemy has wide-range destructive abilities, grouping together becomes fatal.

### Testing Opponents

Against brawlers:

- they usually commit,
- chase,
- pressure directly.

But many fighters:

- hide abilities,
- wait for openings,
- possess devastating ranged options.

### Movement & Terrain

A static battlefield benefits prepared enemies.

Constant movement:

- reveals terrain advantages,
- disrupts enemy planning,
- exposes hidden tactics.

### Prepared Enemies

Mira strongly implies:

> “People who prepare for war prepare for multiple outcomes.”

Then:

> “I have no doubt the Capone Family already has plans prepared for me.”

This is important.

It subtly reinforces:

- Mira is experienced,
- the Capones are dangerous,
- future conflict is inevitable.

### Final Point

She looks toward the crew:

> “So if that happens… I need to know you can protect your own.”

That line should land emotionally.

# Sparring Segment

Mira orders the crew to split into teams of two.

Goal:

- cooperative combat,
- strategy testing,
- exposing weaknesses,
- fun character interactions.

Possible structure:

- rotating matches,
- environmental hazards,
- objectives instead of pure fighting,
- Mira interrupting to critique tactics.

Examples:

- protect an object,
- hold territory,
- isolate an opponent,
- survive against stronger enemies.

# Information Hook — “That Place”

If any of:

- Jack,
- Tray,
- or Midori

report their findings to Mira…

Her demeanor changes slightly.

She becomes more serious.

Then says:

> “If there’s even a chance that place is connected… I want information.”

She instructs the entire crew to investigate discreetly.

Not attack.  
Not confront.

Just learn.

This becomes the next major crew objective.

# Session Themes

## Main Themes

- Unease before escalation
- Tactical growth
- Crew dependency
- Preparation vs instinct
- A world changing in the background

## Hidden Purpose

This session quietly transitions the crew:

- from chaotic adventurers
- into an actual pirate crew preparing for larger-scale conflict.

The mysterious light event should feel like the world itself just shifted slightly.
