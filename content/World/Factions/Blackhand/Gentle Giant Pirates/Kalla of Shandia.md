## Description

**Race:** Skypiean
**Age:** 18
**Glasses:** Always

A young scholar from the cloud island of Shandia, high in the White Sea. Calm and methodical where others are brash — he approaches the world as a puzzle to be solved. Wields **Cane's Arm**, an ancient wooden club carved from a sacred tree branch. Trains daily with it, turning the relic into a weapon worthy of a warrior-scholar.

## Role

Scholar, linguist, and puzzle-solver aboard the [[Gentle Giant Pirates]], a unit of the [[Blackhand]]. Works for [[Blackhand Cane]] as a translator and text decoder under a one-year contract. Though a man of peace, he knows strength is sometimes needed to preserve what's precious.

## Personal Quests

- Find his missing grandfather **Garanth**, a revered elder of the Men of Whispers who vanished without explanation
- Search for the truth of the lost history of the Skypieans, Shandians, and the tales of Mont Blanc Noland
- Counter the anti-peace faction among the Shandians, which grows stronger with Garanth gone

## Backstory

Kalla was born on the cloud island of **Shandia**, high in the White Sea — the firstborn of three siblings in a family caught between tradition and survival.

His **mother, Raisel**, once trained to become one of the **"Men of Whispers"** — the sacred order of historians and lorekeepers among the Shandians. However, she turned away from the path, believing that the pursuit of truth often brought only sorrow. Still, she never stopped Kalla from chasing that same truth.

His **father, Kelhem**, a respected hunter, wasn't one for books or scripture. Boisterous, strong, and slightly goofy, Kelhem taught Kalla the importance of instinct, courage, and resilience — lessons just as vital as the dusty scrolls.

**Menna**, Kalla's younger sister, found joy not in battle but in the hearth — learning to cook, heal, and bring comfort. **Spel**, the youngest, only ten, was a mischievous little stormcloud, full of curiosity and chaos.

But it was **Garanth**, their grandfather — Raisel's father and a revered elder of the Men of Whispers — who left the deepest mark on Kalla.

Garanth wasn't like the others. While the Shandians prepared endlessly for war, he preached **peace** — a dreamer in a land forged by struggle. Most dismissed him as a fool, a man willing to lose if it meant not shedding blood. He often clashed with the council and even other Whisperers. Still, his wisdom ran deep — he held knowledge of the lost history of the Skypieans, Shandians, and even the **tales of Mont Blanc Noland**. His temple, filled with ancient books, crumbling maps, and forgotten dialects, was where Kalla felt most alive.

Kalla spent countless days solving riddles and piecing together old stories Garanth would leave for him. It was during this time he discovered the artifact: **"Cane's Arm,"** a withered yet massive branch carved into a club, once nearly discarded when a sacred tree was cut. Garanth had preserved it in secret. When he vanished, this is what he left behind — along with a letter titled _"A Fool's Errand."_

Garanth's disappearance sent ripples through the already divided Men of Whispers. Some whispered of betrayal. Others mourned. But after months of searching, the elders gave up.

Kalla did not.

Just after his 18th birthday, during a quiet dinner, the news came: the search for Garanth was officially ended. Kalla, unable to accept it, took the ancient club, some gifts from his family, and stood at the edge of the sky island. With the sea of clouds beneath him and fire in his heart, he leapt — falling from island to island, hurt and hungry but alive.

Eventually, he landed on a remote island of beasts and forest. He was stranded. Until a ship arrived — bearing a **Jolly Roger**.

Desperate and out of options, Kalla called out. The pirates took him aboard. Their captain? A giant of a man — the infamous **[[Blackhand Cane]]**. They fed him, gave him time to recover. Then Blackhand asked a simple question: _"Who are you?"_

Kalla replied calmly, **"I am Kalla of Shandia, a scholar of the White Sea."**

Intrigued, Blackhand handed Kalla some old scriptures. Within hours, Kalla was lost in symbols and forgotten languages — deciphering patterns, tracing lost names. When Blackhand returned, he watched quietly.

"You're still at it, huh?" Blackhand grinned. "A pirate always pays his debts. You owe me your life. So here's the deal — work for me for one year. Help me uncover what's written in these old texts. In return, I'll give you enough berries and a ship to go find whatever you're looking for. Deal?"

Kalla considered for a moment.
"Only if I can continue searching for my people."

"As you wish," Blackhand said. "Welcome aboard."

---

With Garanth gone, the anti-peace faction among the Shandians grows stronger, pushing for all-out war. But Kalla carries the weight of a lost history, a vanished grandfather, and a thousand riddles waiting to be solved. Somewhere in the Blue Sea lies the truth — and he'll find it, even if he has to read the world itself.
