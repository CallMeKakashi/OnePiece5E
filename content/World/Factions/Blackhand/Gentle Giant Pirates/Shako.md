## Description

A mantis shrimp fishman — brilliant multicolored carapace inherited from his mother, devastating punching strength, and his father's resourcefulness and mechanical hands. Timid and reserved by nature, but monstrously strong when it counts. His strikes shatter guard after guard; his shells take blows like steel.

## Role

Shipwright of the [[Gentle Giant Pirates]], a unit of the [[Blackhand]]. Recruited by [[Renzo]] from underground fighting pits and freed from his contract with the promoter **Boro**. Builds and maintains ships for the crew; carries ship tools at his side and fists that could shatter steel.

## Personal Quests

- Provide for his younger brother **Ebi** back on CraggHammer Island
- Live on his own terms — no longer a battered fighter for someone else's profit
- Master his craft as a shipwright on the open seas

## Backstory

### Origins & Family

Shako was born on **CraggHammer Island**, a small, remote island far from the main Fishman Island, deep in the Grand Line's unpredictable waters. Unlike the famed Fishman Island under the sea, CraggHammer was founded generations ago by crustacean-type fishmen who wanted a more peaceful, secluded life. Over time, it became a tight-knit community known for its strong, armor-like shells and shipbuilding traditions.

Although governed by their own **Parliament of Claws**, they still pledged loyalty to King Neptune and Queen Otohime, paying tribute and following the Fishman Island's laws in broad strokes.

**Father: Gansai** — a mighty Japanese spider crab fishman, famous for his long, spindly arms and immense reach. Gansai worked as a deep-sea salvager and sometimes took risky surface trips to collect rare metals and treasures for extra coin.

**Mother: Rumi** — a gentle mantis shrimp fishwoman, known for her beautiful multicolored carapace and delicate craftsmanship with coral jewelry.

From these two, Shako inherited his mother's brilliant colors and devastating punching strength, but his father's resourcefulness and mechanical hands.

### Early Childhood

Shako had a peaceful, if humble, upbringing. His mother often taught him about coral shaping and delicate craftwork, while his father regaled him with thrilling — if terrifying — stories of the surface world.

When Shako was only 12, his mother **Rumi** died giving birth to his younger brother **Ebi**, a shy but clever shrimp-type fishman. Soon after, Gansai vanished on one of his surface salvage trips, leaving Shako and Ebi to fend for themselves.

### Struggling for Survival

At just 16, Shako became the family's sole provider. He started as a handyman and junior shipwright, working for a local shipyard on CraggHammer Island, repairing fishing boats and surfacing dinghies. Despite his shy, reserved nature, his mantis shrimp strength made him invaluable when hammering thick plates or shaping hard coral keels.

However, it wasn't enough. One day, a shady underground boxing promoter spotted Shako's potential. Desperate for money to feed Ebi, Shako agreed to join underground fights in the seedy docks of **Yobatsuru Port**, a nearby surface island known for illegal fights and black-market auctions.

Though timid, Shako's natural strength made him a formidable opponent — his strikes shattered guard after guard, his shells took blows like steel. The crowds loved him, but the promoter, **Boro**, saw Shako as just another pawn.

Boro began forcing him to throw fights, sabotage opponents, and even permanently injure other fighters to rig bets and earn more. The money was good, but Shako's spirit grew heavy.

### The Unexpected Visitor — Renzo

One night, during a particularly brutal match, a mysterious fishman watched from the shadows. Rumors whispered that he was a right-hand man of a powerful pirate captain.

After the fight, the man approached Shako, not as another promoter or fight fan, but with genuine interest. He introduced himself as **[[Renzo]]**, an old but burly hammerhead shark fishman.

Renzo wasn't impressed by Shako's fists — he was captivated by the sturdy, precise hands that had built and repaired small surface ships in Yobatsuru and CraggHammer. Renzo saw not a pit fighter, but a true craftsman trapped in a bloodstained ring.

### The Choice

Renzo offered Shako a way out: join his pirate crew as a shipwright, traveling the seas, building and maintaining ships, and finally living on his own terms.

Renzo even paid off Boro... Boro was not happy but he also couldn't refuse Renzo, freeing Shako from his bloody contract. At first, Shako hesitated — he worried for Ebi. But after heartfelt talks, Ebi encouraged him to go, promising to keep the workshop running and to wait for his big brother's return.

### A New Chapter

With tears in his eyes and a trembling, but determined heart, Shako left CraggHammer and Yobatsuru behind. For the first time, he could build, travel, and fight as he chose — no longer just a battered fighter for someone else's profit.

Now, this timid yet monstrously strong mantis shrimp fishman sails the seas, ship tools at his side, fists that could shatter steel ready if needed.
