## Description

A round white wolf mink. Energetic, restless, incapable of sitting still. Warm-hearted and impulsive — the kind who eats first and asks questions never. His cooking carries the martial discipline of Zou's Moonlight Path Temple, blending combat flow with culinary art.

## Role

Cook of the [[Lunarfolds]]. Trained in the Warrior Recipes of Zou's Moonlight Path Temple under Master Tsubaki. Devil Fruit: [[Yuki Yuki no Mi — Model Yeti]]. Frost powers not yet fully mastered.

## Personal Quests

- Master the winter storm inside him so he can return to Zou
- Reunite with Chiru and Master Tsubaki
- Learn the fate of Pasha, the lion mink who fled the temple

## Backstory

### The Wolf Who Would Not Sit Still

"Roma! Will you sit down for ONE breath?" — Shibamu

Zou's morning bells rang across the misted cliffs, echoing between the ancient stone halls of the Moonlight Path Temple. The young minks sat cross-legged, backs straight, eyes closed— a sea of serenity.

Except for one.

A round white wolf mink pup bounced on his cushion like it was a drum.

"Roma…" hissed Master Tsubaki without opening his eyes, "the mind is a pond. Still it, and it will reflect truth."

"But Master…" Roma whispered, "what if I don't WANT to see the pond? What if I want to see what's cooking in the kitchen?"

A few minks snorted. Someone coughed to hide a laugh.

Chiru, the tiny rabbit mink sitting beside him, nudged him with her foot.

"Focus, snowball," she whispered. "We're supposed to achieve inner harmony."

Roma crossed his arms. "I'll achieve it after breakfast."

Master Tsubaki sighed. "You cannot outrun enlightenment."

Roma grinned. "I can try."

---

### The Daylord and the Nightwarden

That evening, the temple bustled—a rare moment when Zou's two rulers visited together. The Daylord Solfern, proud and stern; the Nightwarden Shiorin, calm but sharp-tongued. Their arguments were legendary.

Roma, of course, snuck into the rafters to watch.

"…I told you the border scouts need reinforcement!" barked the Solfern.

"And I told you they need rest, not brutes," replied the Shiorin coolly.

But when the temple cooks placed steaming bowls of Moonbroth before them, the rulers fell silent.

Shiorin inhaled deeply. "This smell brings back seasons long gone."

The Solfern nodded, tension melting away. "We used to share meals like this before we ruled anything."

Roma watched them quietly—two forces of nature softened by a single dish.

Respect. Awe. Purpose bloomed inside him.

Later that night, Master Tsubaki found Roma staring at the empty bowls left behind.

"You saw it, didn't you?" the old tiger mink asked.

Roma nodded. "They were fighting. And then they ate and… they weren't."

Tsubaki sat beside him. "Food is a miracle, Roma. A bridge between hearts. You feed the stomach to calm the body… and you feed the body to calm the spirit."

Roma blinked up at him. "So cooking is like monk magic?"

Tsubaki smiled. "If done with intention? Yes. It binds, heals, reminds, forgives."

Roma looked at the empty dishes again. "I want to learn to do that. I want to make people feel safe."

"Then you must learn the Warrior Recipes," Tsubaki said.

---

### The Secret Recipes of Zou

Training changed after that.

Roma still tripped, still burned rice, still set himself on fire—but he laughed through it.

Master Tsubaki guided him through the Warrior Recipes, each tied to a martial flow.

"Stir with the hips. Strike with the hips," Tsubaki instructed.

Roma swirled the ladle, stumbled, and lit his sleeve on fire.

Chiru clapped. "Aaaaaand he's cooking himself again!"

Roma patted out the flames. "This is fine. I'm fine."

And with every dish, the monks found themselves warmed in ways they couldn't explain.

---

### The Lion in the Shadows

One evening, Roma and Chiru wandered down a corridor they had never seen before. The air felt heavy.

A faded sign read:

**WARNING — RESTRICTED AREA — TRAINEES NOT ALLOWED**

They turned to leave—but a shadow moved behind them.

A towering lion mink stepped into the lantern light. His mane flowed as though carried by an unseen wind.

He opened his palms—revealing a rosary, and berries at its center.

"Here. Something for you."

Roma and Chiru hesitated, then took them.

A senior monk arrived, pale and terrified.

"**PASHA!** Stay away from them! You are not allowed contact!"

Pasha bowed deeply. As he turned, markings etched across his body glowed faintly.

He disappeared into a dark room.

Days later, chaos erupted—Pasha had fled, injuring many monks.

Both the Solfern and Shiorin came in person.

The Shiorin, a tall snow‑leopard mink with pale, frost‑patched fur, stepped forward first. Her voice was low, but grief cracked through every word.

"Poor boy… if only you had listened to me Solfern, … it wouldn't have come to this."

The Solfern—a broad‑shouldered fox mink whose tail bristled like a storm—snarled under his breath.

"Save your pity, Shiorin. That beast was a danger to everyone. Good riddance he's gone."

Shiorin's ears flattened, her tone sharpening. "Do not call him that. Pasha was one of ours. A mink of Zou. A child of this land."

"A child?" the Solfern barked. "He nearly tore through half the temple! If the seals had broken sooner, we'd be mournin' bodies, not arguin' here."

Shiorin stepped closer, eyes icy. "And whose fault is it that he lost control? You pushed him. You treated him like a weapon, not a soul. He needed guidance—compassion—not chains and isolation."

"He needed containment," the Solfern snapped. "A lion with that much power runnin' free? Madness. Now he's the world's problem, not ours."

The Shiorin growled softly. "You can't just leave him to die out there. He's alone. Confused. And still one of our people. We need to send someone to look for him."

"Oh, I'll send people," the Solfern spat. "But not to coddle him. We're better off without him, and I'll not risk more minks for the sake of that creature."

The two rulers stood nose‑to‑nose, breath steaming in the cold air, a storm of grief and anger crackling between them.

Roma and Chiru watched from behind a column—silent, trembling.

They had never seen their leaders divided like this.

They had never felt fear this heavy in the air.

"This will endanger the world," the Nightwarden said quietly.

Roma and Chiru never forgot the lion's calm eyes.

---

### The Devil's Dessert

"Roma… what did you just eat?" — Kara

During a supply run, Roma wandered off and found a shattered pirate hull lodged in the folds of Zunesha.

Inside, a chest lay frozen solid.

"Oooooh. Ice cream treasure," Roma whispered.

He cracked it open—revealing a strange shimmering fruit.

"Probably sweet."

Behind him, Chiru screamed:

"ROMA DON'T YOU EAT THA—"

CHOMP.

Roma blinked. "…Tingly."

Frost crept up his arms.

Chiru grabbed him. "You idiot! That's a Devil Fruit!"

---

### The Courtyard Event

That night during meditation, a spider landed on Roma.

He yelped.

Frost exploded outward.

Minks screamed. Half the courtyard froze.

Master Tsubaki approached through the frost.

"Roma… you carry a winter your spirit cannot yet hold."

"I didn't mean to—"

"I know."

Tsubaki placed a paw on his head. "But intent cannot stop destruction. You must leave, until you master your storm."

Roma cried silently. "Master… I don't want to go."

"Then return stronger."

That night, he left Zou without saying goodbye.

---

### A Wandering Cook

Roma traveled. He froze taverns. He thawed them. He was fired endlessly.

Eventually he found work on a luxury cruise ship—wearing a mask to hide he was a mink.

Panic hit the kitchen one day.

"Blackhand Cane is on board!"

"Someone cook him something incredible!"

Roma stepped forward. "I can do it."

---

### An Intriguing Dish

Blackhand Cane sat alone at the center of the grand dining hall, a mountain of a man with scars like map lines across his skin. Candlelight danced along the metal rings in his coat and the blackened fingers that earned him his infamous title. The chatter in the room died the moment he lifted his spoon.

He took a slow, heavy bite of Roma's dish.

The entire hall stilled.

Cane chewed once. Twice. His expression unreadable.

Then—

"…Bring me the cook."

The head chef practically shoved Roma forward. Roma stumbled out from the kitchen, wearing his plain mask, his tail stiff with fear.

Cane leaned forward, the light catching the weathered iron of his jawline. His voice rolled out like the deep churn of old tidewater.

"Aye… this stew o' yours burns hot and cold in the same spoon. Might be the best damn thing I've tasted in a long, long while."

Roma puffed out his chest, hands on his hips.

"Damn right!"

A few nobles gasped. A few pirates snorted laughter.

Cane barked a rough, thunderous laugh and pointed at the chair across from him.

"Sit. Name's Blackhand. Me friends call me Cane. And you, Roma… are me friend already. Call me whatever ye like. I like yer spirit."

Roma hesitated, then plopped down in the chair like he owned it. Cane smirked.

He tapped the table once—an echoing, deliberate sound.

"Ye hide behind a mask… but a true cook hides nothin'. Not his hands. Not his heart."

Roma froze. "Hey! How'd you know?"

Cane lifted one blackened finger.

"I could lie an' say I've got the eyes o' a hawk… but truth be told, pup—yer disguise might've fooled a blind seagull if yer paws weren't pokin' out the damn boots."

Roma stared at his feet, horrified. "Oh come on…"

Cane rose to his full height, coat billowing dramatically behind him as though the ocean wind itself pushed it.

"So what say ye? Join me band o' pirates, lad. See the world. Fill it with flavors it ain't never dreamed of."

Roma ripped the mask off in one smooth motion.

"I say YES!!"

Before Cane could reply, the dining room doors SLAMMED open with a violent crash.

Pirates stormed in—rowdy, chaotic, drunk, starved, and already chanting:

"BANQUET! BANQUET! BANQUET! BANQUET!"

Cane pinched the bridge of his nose.

"Alright, ye sorry sons o' bitches…"

He turned toward the nobles and commoners bunched up in the corner, trembling like frightened goats.

"What're ye waitin' for? Take yer seats! Let's have ourselves a proper banquet!"

The hall erupted into anarchy.

Pirates dragged tables together. Chefs scrambled. Wine spilled like waterfalls. Nobles reluctantly took seats beside tattooed marauders. A violinist fainted. Someone started juggling bread. A pair of cooks arm-wrestled on top of the dessert table.

Roma stood in the center of it all—watching pirates, commoners, nobles, servants, and mercenaries dance, shout, drink, laugh, and feast like a single united storm.

A swirling, impossible family.

A sight so warm and wild it made his chest ache.

He inhaled deeply—wine and roasted meat and ocean-salt air tangling together.

In that moment, Roma knew.

**His future with this crew would be unlike anything he'd ever seen.
A life of chaos, flavor, warmth…
and belonging.**
