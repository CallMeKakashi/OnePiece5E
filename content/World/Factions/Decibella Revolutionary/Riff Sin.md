## Description

A young man with red messy hair, leather vest, and a red scarf. He holds a guitar that has been modified into shooting rubber rounds that make little noise when used. Talks in whispers but excited and fast talker.

## Role

Rebel fighter in the [[Decibella Revolutionary]], leads the [[Cadence]] cell. Cousin of [[Synth]], a member of the [[Soundless 5]].

## Personal Quests

## Backstory

Riff still remembers the Emperor's Hall by its silence.
He was a child then, standing stiff beside his family as his cousin stepped forward and was named one of the _Soundless Five_. The hall swallowed applause before it could exist. Riff leaned toward his father and whispered, _"Why do we have to bow? Why can't we speak?"_

The answer came as a strike across his face.
Later that night, through half-closed doors, he heard his parents speak of disappointment—how Riff lacked the stillness, the obedience, the emptiness required to be Soundless. Even then, sound clung to him like breath.

Years later, as a teenager, Riff made himself a problem in the noble district. He played his guitar loud and sharp, notes spilling where silence was demanded. A commoner girl stopped to dance, laughing freely. The nobles recoiled in disgust. Guards moved to drag Riff down, hands rough, faces sneering—until chaos scattered the moment and he vanished into the crowd, heart pounding, grin unrepentant.

Not long after, the slums became home.
There, Riff ran wild with Cadence—music echoing through broken streets, joy carved out between hunger and fear. But the laughter never drowned out what they saw: beatings in alleys, children punished for noise, lives crushed for existing too loudly. Sound, he realized, was rebellion.

The rebellion base was colder, darker—but honest.
Riff played for the wounded, for the angry, for those who had forgotten how it felt to feel. Cadence stood beside him, steady and burning, their rhythm in sync even when words failed.

Then came the last night.
Cadence strapped her gear on in silence, Keera waiting at the tunnel mouth. Riff tried to joke, tried to play something light—but the notes wouldn't come out right. Cadence squeezed his shoulder once before turning away.

The sound of her footsteps leaving stayed with him longer than any song.
