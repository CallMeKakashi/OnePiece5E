## 🌀 **Session 4 – “The Calm Before the Storm”**

### 🌅 **Evening — Aboard the Ship, Post-Escape**

The sun is dipping low as the crew begins to stir, groggy and battered but alive. The events of Craw Island play like a chaotic dream — the destroyed port, Grinjaw’s sunken ship, and the towering figure of **Vice Admiral Horus** looming over them before it all went black.

As they reorient themselves aboard their vessel, **Veyl** steps onto the deck, stretching with a yawn and flashing a sharp grin:

> **"Alright, here’s the deal. Below deck is off-limits — Serica’s crashing there. She sleeps like a log and wakes like a shotgun, so don’t test her."**
> 
> **"The rest of you? Find a corner, claim a hammock, argue over the sail — whatever. I’m just the bodyguard. Where you go, we follow. That’s the order."**

Night falls, giving the crew time to rest, plan, or recover from the serum-induced chaos.

---

### 🌞 **Morning — “Extra, Extra!”**

The next day breaks with sea breeze and sunlight. The gentle creak of the ship is suddenly interrupted by **a sharp thump on deck** — a rolled-up newspaper lands with a splash of ink and feather fluff. A few **Big News Morgan pelicans** squawk overhead before vanishing into the skies.

**Veyl** picks it up, reads silently, and chuckles.

> **"Well... guess you’re famous now."**

### 📸 **Exclusive Interview: Vice Admiral Horus Comments**

A stark black-and-white photo shows **Vice Admiral Horus** amidst the rubble at Craw Island, standing motionless as **chunks of the earth look bitten clean through**, his towering form framed by destruction.

His quote is printed prominently:

> **“They got lucky. Luck runs out.”**  
> **“If they value what little power they’ve scraped together, they’ll stay out of the Grand Line.”**  
> **“I’ve been reassigned to G-14. There’s a disturbance nearby. If they cross my path again... I’ll show them what the world government really is.”**

## 🌊 **Post-Newspaper Scene – “Hunting Time”**

As the crew finishes reading the newspaper, a mix of reactions ripple across the deck — pride, disbelief, maybe even a little fear.

**Veyl** stretches again, tossing the paper aside.

> **"So... where do you lot keep your food and water stash?"**

A beat. Someone in the crew looks confused.

> **"Didn’t you bring the supplies? You _did_ grab the crates when you rescued us... right?"**

Veyl blinks. Freezes. His grin drops.

> **"...I had to haul four knocked-out pirates, one bruised ego, and a whole lotta guilt off that island before a vice admiral turned me into toothpaste. I grabbed what _you_ had on you. Not a single crate. You’re lucky your pants made it."**

He glances toward the lower deck. Visibly gulps.

> **"...Serica’s _not_ great when she’s hungry. Like... _weaponizing cutlery_ not-great."**

He takes a sharp breath, cracks his knuckles, and walks to the edge of the ship.

His fingers start to twitch—faster and faster—until his **whole hands blur**, vibrating with unnatural speed.

He plants one boot on the railing.

> **"Alright, crew... get ready."**  
> **"It’s hunting time."**

The sea rumbles. The waves part ever so slightly.  
A glint of scales. A rush of water.

From the depths, a **massive sea beast** bursts upward — jagged fins, snapping jaws, and too many eyes to count.
