# Grand Line Tribune — 12th Moon, 1478 (Morning)

*"Truth Rides the Waves" — Price: 2,000 Beli*

## VIRETH STRIKES G–55: VICE ADMIRALS SHATTERED

In a stunning assault last night, the notorious leader of the mercenary order, The Sixfolds. Skyblade Vireth infiltrated Marine Base G–5-5 in the South Blue.

According to shaken survivors, Vireth disabled the entire Vice Admiral contingent stationed at the base before locating and looking for a high-priority asset:

**Cline D. Davis** — better known worldwide as Cline the Plague.

Witnesses claim Vireth was not acting alone. Reports confirm the presence of Elizabeth Marrow, also known as Guillotine Liz, former leader of the mercenary order The Sixfolds. Together, the two carved a path through the stronghold and escaped before reinforcements arrived.

Marine HQ has confirmed that due to this attack, Vireth's bounty has been restored and significantly raised, as has Elizabeth Marrow's. Updated posters are being distributed across all the Blues.

A brief statement from Headquarters reads:

> Base G–5-5 has suffered catastrophic losses.  
> The fugitives escaped. Their threat level is raised.  
> All bounty updates are now active.

## SURVIVORS CLAIM BLACKHAND UNIT SEEN ON CALLISTO

**Corven Twins Sighted**

The rescued personnel from the destroyed Callisto Research Facility are reporting startling claims. Multiple survivors insist they saw members of the infamous Blackhand Unit, previously last seen near Spirit Cliff in the East Blue.

According to testimonies, the pirates were not alone. They were accompanied by several new, unidentified individuals who fought throughout the collapsing facility.

Adding to the confusion, two more figures were reportedly present: the Corven Twins, formerly of the mercenary order The Sixfolds.

Marine officials have confirmed the identities, and new bounties related to the incident have been released separately.

## MUGEN INDUSTRIES FINDS UNEXPECTED RELIEF AFTER CALLISTO DISASTER

Following the Callisto collapse, Mugen Industries — still under scrutiny after the recent Enforcer-Armour malfunction scandal — has found some positive momentum.

Commodore Briggs, a major Mugen shareholder, claimed:

> Those malfunctions weren't accidents.  
> They were sabotaged by the very pirates who destroyed Callisto.

While some agree with the Commodore's assessment, others dismiss his statements as self-serving attempts to shift responsibility away from Mugen's engineering division.

Still, with the facility now ruined, critics worry that valuable evidence may have been permanently lost.

## FOUR SEAS IN TURMOIL AS BLACKHAND CANE'S DISAPPEARANCE GOES PUBLIC

Ever since confirmation of the pardoned pirate-lord Blackhand Cane's disappearance, tensions across the world have escalated.

Reports include:

- Pirate crews clashing for territory vacuums
- Mafia syndicates are accelerating long-standing conflicts
- Merchant routes are delayed due to rising ambushes
- Former Blackhand affiliates have been silent.

The Shadows, a bounty hunter Guild has formally announced its intent to claim all remaining Blackhand-related bounties.

Guild Spokesman Jera stated:

> It's open season for his people. We'll claim every bounty.

## DIRE NEWS FROM DRIFTROOT — DETAILS LIMITED

Communication with Driftroot Island remains intermittent following an unconfirmed event late last night. Early reports from passing merchant ships describe:

- The island has been put under quarantine.
- Strange clouds originating from the heart of the island
- Local wildlife in a state of frenzy

The Tribune's eastern desk is preparing a full report for the later Edition.
