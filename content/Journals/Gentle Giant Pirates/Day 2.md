# Day 2 — Driftwood, near Grey Mire

## Setting

- **When:** Morning landing through night watch
- **Where:** West dock of Driftwood; Grey Mire bandit camp; camp outside town
- **Author:** Unnamed crew member (first person)

## Entry

New crew is a little awkward. We kept ourselves for the most part while on the sea. Well, maybe it'll get better in time.

We landed at the west dock of Driftwood this morning. The skies were gray, but not in a bad way—just kind of quiet, like the town itself. We came looking for [[Dravos]]. He's been missing, last seen around pirates. That usually means something went wrong. Or maybe he went looking for wrong himself. Either way, we're not leaving without answers.

Driftwood isn't the kind of place that stares long at strangers. People here see all kinds come through, heading for the Grand Line. That works in our favor. They don't look twice, which means we can move without making ripples.

That's when an old soul of a man showed up—like he still had breath, but was weighed down by something unseen. Said he needed help, for a child's sake. Five berries. We said yes, even though it's barely anything. I think I would've helped her for free, but no one said it out loud. Sometimes you just know when something matters.

Turns out she went to the ruins of the Old City, picking flowers. Came back sick. Really sick. Her throat, hands, and feet had old burn scars—ones that healed a long time ago but still told stories. The city burned down years back. Orphanage too. She used to visit it—maybe she lived there once. She still goes to lay flowers. Grieving. That's a heavy thing for someone so small. She probably breathed in old ash. Captain called it ash-lung fever. We knew we had to act fast.

While the Captain helped her, we tracked a guy heading toward the marshes. Word was Dravos had gotten in with the Grey Marrow bandits. If that's true… he's in real trouble. No one out here cares about bandits—not the guards, not the marines. This part of the world looks away. Even the "silk court," whoever they are.

We found the bandits hiding in Grey Mire. We waited, then moved. Akira ambushed a guard while Captain and Vex took another. I hung back, watching. When the fight started, everything felt too fast. Akira and Shaka missed their marks. I threw my great club. It landed clean—killed him outright. My hands shook after. I didn't show it.

The other bandit we knocked out. When he woke up, we got him talking. Turns out Dravos wasn't one of them—not really. They didn't respect him. Beat him, tossed him outside the ruins like trash. Their leader's a guy named Nix—the one calling shots. Vex scared the name out of him. Captain and Akira tried, but I think the bandit could see through them.

While that was happening, I dragged the dead one's body behind the cliff. Out of view. Didn't feel right, but it needed doing. Captain told the bandit to free the gorillas they'd captured. He did, crying a little when he saw his dead friend. Then he ran. Maybe he'll make it out. Maybe not.

The gorillas were doped. Angry. Not normal. Their fur was dyed red and green from whatever they were injected with. We found a syringe marked "6 6." Akira recognized the make—something tied to the old captain. Some ghost from a past mission. Everything's connected out here in ways I don't always understand.

Then the fighting started again. One gorilla tore through a bandit like he was paper. Another tried to run. Captain shot his leg. Akira finished it—headshot. Clean. Gorillas and us took the rest. Camp was empty after that. But not quiet.

Gorillas didn't calm down. They were wild with fear and pain. One got hurt. The other was still full of whatever drug they gave it. Captain tricked it with drugged bananas, got it to sleep, and released it further away in the wilderness. We patched up the hurt one too, but he didn't make it. Died not long after.

We found more loot—50,000 berries. Den Den Mushi too. One looked different—black coat, weird vibe. We found a note: orders to inject the gorillas and send back data in a week's time. Someone's running experiments, or maybe it was Nix himself. We'll trace it back.

Burned the bodies. Said some words for them. Even if they were bandits. Even if they hurt people. I don't know if it helps, but it's better than silence.

We need medicine for the girl. Tomorrow we head to Egg Bank. The Old City is close too. Maybe Dravos is still there. Maybe not. We'll find out.

Tonight we rest. I'm up in a tree, watching. Captain's in the tent. Shaka's in the dark corner of the camp. Akira's curled behind some logs. I set up the tone dial—just in case something moves in the night. I don't like surprises when we're all this tired.

I'm still figuring out how to write these. I guess I don't always know how I feel until I see it written down. But I know this: we can't give up on Dravos. And we can't let that girl die.
