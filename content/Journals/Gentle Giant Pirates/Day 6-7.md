# **Driftroot Isle → South Blue**

The Marine vessel was never meant to be quiet.

But that night, the creaking of its hull sounded almost respectful — as if the ship itself sensed it was carrying something that should not be disturbed.

“You hear that, little thing?”

Two Marines approached from opposite ends of the deck, boots heavy, careless, confident in the way authority often is when it believes itself untouchable. Between them, a small monkey clung to a length of rope, fur bristling, teeth bared.

One Marine crouched, nudging him with the barrel of a rifle.

“Pirate’s pet, huh?” he laughed.  
“Bet it squeals.”

The monkey screeched in answer.

The lantern light shifted.

A shadow swallowed it whole.

One step.

**BOOM.**

The deck shuddered violently as one Marine was launched through the air, slamming spine-first into the brig bars hard enough to bend iron. The second Marine barely managed to turn before a massive boot caved into his chest, sending him skidding across the deck like loose cargo.

Fenris stepped forward.

Nine feet and seven inches tall. Scarred muscle coiled tight beneath his skin. Blood ran freely from his knuckles, but his breathing was slow — controlled.

The monkey scrambled to his leg.

“Don’t,” Fenris said, voice low and even,  
“pick on my friend.”

The Marines did not laugh again.

---

They did not overwhelm him with numbers.

They did not rush him.

They brought **precision**.

Steel flashed. Commands were issued once — quietly.

Fenris went down.

When consciousness returned, it came with pain so sharp it blurred the edges of the world. His ribs screamed with every breath. Blood filled his mouth. The deck beneath him was cold and slick.

Vex was shrieking.

The locket around Fenris’ neck — always sealed, always heavy — was now soaked through with blood. With frantic instinct, the monkey bit down on the injector and drove it home into Fenris’ chest.

Footsteps approached.

A Marine stood over him, rapier immaculate, posture perfect. His expression was calm. Professional.

“There is only one form of Justice,” the Marine murmured.  
“Pure. Unstoppable. Strength.”

The blade flashed.

Darkness swallowed the world.

---

The Marine turned away.

Behind him—

**something inhaled.**

Wood creaked. Iron groaned.

Fenris rose.

Bigger. Broader.

The blue tattoos etched into his arms burned crimson as blood-red fluid surged beneath his skin like living ink. Veins bulged grotesquely. His eyes ignited scarlet, and the vessels along his brow throbbed violently.

The Marine froze.

“…Impossible.”

Fenris’ breath came out wrong — wet, feral, heavy with something old and furious.

Vex chittered.

Fenris looked at him.

Even like this —  
**he knew his friend.**

The Marine did not finish the fight.

He ran.

And Drez, already gone, never saw what he left behind.

---

Driftroot Isle was already burning when the crew arrived.

Smoke curled through the canopy. Bells rang from the eastern quarter. The mist — thin, silvery, wrong — crept through alleyways and along rooftops.

“There,” Shako said quietly, eyes narrowing.

A blonde Marine stood in the street, blade raised, facing a massive silhouette hunched beneath a torn awning.

“I see your backup has arrived,” the Marine called out.

He turned, locking eyes with Shako.

Then rushed.

The street cracked as Fenris stepped between them, stone fracturing beneath his weight.

“Do not,” Fenris growled, voice layered with something wet and dangerous,  
“touch my crew.”

Malik instinctively shifted his stance.  
Kalla reached for his weapon.  
Even Facade paused.

A voice broke the moment.

“…Leon?”

The Marine faltered.

“…Goru?”

The punch came without warning.

Leon staggered as Goru struck him square in the jaw.

“What the hell was that for?!” Leon shouted.

“For poisoning my island!” Goru roared.  
“For the mist! The rivers! My father — Laziel — working with Marine officer Drez! You helped them!”

Leon’s face drained of color.

“I didn’t,” he said quickly. “I was confined to the ship. They told me it was aid. Training.”

Nyx stepped forward, expression unreadable.  
“Then you trained blind.”

Malik folded his arms.  
“Prove you weren’t complicit.”

Leon swallowed hard.  
“…Tell me what to do.”

---

Nyx led the crew through hidden passages beneath Eggbank. Facade moved with terrifying focus, rewiring dispersal mechanisms while explaining in clipped precision.

“This will aerosolize the cure,” he said.  
“There will be losses. But the western docks can be spared.”

“And the nobles?” Kalla asked.

Facade didn’t look up.  
“They will breathe the same air.”

“Do it,” Malik said.

When the cure spread, screams echoed — then softened.

Flint gasped violently, body convulsing. Robb clawed at the ground.

Then they breathed.

“…We’re back,” Robb whispered.

---

Fenris stood near the docks, scrubbing dried blood from his arms. The red still pulsed faintly beneath his tattoos.

“You always been able to do… that?” Shako asked.

“I hoped I never would,” Fenris replied.

“I saw your eyes,” Kalla said.

“I know where you are,” Fenris answered.  
“I hear you breathe.”

“That’s unsettling,” Malik muttered.

Vex climbed onto Fenris’ shoulder.

“I won’t hurt you,” Fenris said.  
“Not any of you.”

Facade adjusted his glasses.  
“Statistically reassuring.”

---

Near the clinic, Sana froze.

Leon stopped.

They stared — five years collapsing into nothing — and embraced.

Nyx watched from a distance, jaw tight.

“They were orphans,” he said flatly. “Before the fire.”

“And after?” Shako asked.

Nyx exhaled.  
“After… everyone chose something.”

---

That night, the people of Driftroot gathered — nobles and commoners alike — around shared fires.

Flint and Robb sat awkwardly among them.

“Never thought I’d survive poisoning,” Flint muttered.

“Never thought nobles would share food,” Robb replied.

---

“Crew name,” Shako said later. “We need one.”

“Aren’t we just the Blackhand Pirates?” Fenris replied.

Sana smiled.

“How about the **Gentle Giant Pirates**?”

Fenris laughed — startled, genuine.

“They always feared me,” he said softly. “Never bothered learning my name.”

“That changes now,” Shako said.

---

Leon left at dawn.

“I’ll find Drez,” he swore.

He saluted — not like a Marine.

Like a man.

Goru stood at the docks.

“I’ll take the title,” he said. “If the people will have me.”

“They will,” Fenris said. “If you protect them.”

Nyx said nothing.

But he stayed.

---

When Dravos smiled, Malik immediately said,  
“No.”

“Hear me out.”

“Physics is crying,” Shako groaned.

“Final Technique,” Dravos declared.  
“**Thousand Mile Tunnel.**”

The Red Line screamed.

The **Rustwing** launched skyward — then fell.

“**DRAVOS WHAT DID YOU DO?!**” Fenris roared.

“SORRY, CAPTAIN! SAVE MEEEE!”

They fell into the South Blue laughing, screaming, alive.

And so the **Gentle Giant Pirates** were born —

Not from fear.

But from choosing each other anyway.
