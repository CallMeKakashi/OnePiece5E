L# Revised Session Outline — _Ashes Before the Storm_

With Bob absent, the session becomes much tighter and character-focused around:

- **Jack** — investigation / underworld angle
- **Midori** — outsider entering the crew/world

This actually works well because both characters naturally contrast each other:

- Jack = grounded, observant, suspicious
- Midori = new blood, emotional uncertainty, adapting

You can structure the session so they slowly become forced into cooperating.

---

# Session Structure

## PART 1 — Clinic Morning (Midori Focus)

### Scene

Midori wakes in the clinic.

Linus is checking on Baptiste’s injured friend.

This opening should breathe a little.

### Important Goals

- Ground Midori emotionally.
- Let him ask questions naturally.
- Introduce the current state of the island.
- Transition him toward the crew.

### Linus Dialogue Beats

- “Let him rest.”
- “I’ll come by daily for a while.”
- “Don’t worry about the money. A friend of Baptiste is a friend of mine.”

Then:

- asks Midori where he plans to go next
- mentions Baptiste’s crew is doing work around town
- suggests staying with them temporarily

### Important Addition

Since Bob is absent:  
Linus can specifically say:

> “Baptiste mentioned one of the boys didn’t show this morning. Means they’re probably short-handed.”

This elegantly explains Bob’s absence in-universe.

---

# PART 2 — Tavern Briefing (Jack Focus)

## Scene Setup

Jack arrives at the tavern.

Present:

- Tray
- Tom
- A few tired patrons
- Baptiste and Roma preparing to leave

Tom comes downstairs carrying clinic reports.

He gathers:

- Jack
- Tray

Then Baptiste returns with Midori shortly after.

---

# Tom’s Discovery

Tom reveals:

- The monsters were mutated by concentrated Lunafang exposure.
- Same substance from the West Blue smuggling operations.

### Tone

Less heroic.  
More grim realization.

### Key Line

> “This isn’t some street poison anymore.”

Then:

> “If someone’s making these things on purpose… this island’s already in trouble.”

---

# The Missing Farm Lead

Tom explains:

- A farm in West Town has gone silent.
- No one investigated.
- Fear’s spreading.

Because Bob is absent:

## Tom Adjusts the Assignment

Instead of splitting the group:

He sends:

- Jack
- Midori
- Tray

together.

---

# Important Dynamic — Why Jack and Midori Work Together

Tom deliberately pairs them because:

- Jack knows the streets.
- Midori is capable but unknown.
- Tray is there to stabilize the group socially.

Tom might say:

> “You two are different kinds of useful.”

or

> “One sees people. One sees danger. Figure out which is which on the road there.”

---

# Baptiste Exit Scene

Baptiste leaves shortly after.

Important:  
He should briefly acknowledge Midori before leaving.

### Example

> “Don’t die before I get back.”
> 
> _pause_
> 
> “Would be inconvenient.”

Classic dry Baptiste energy.

---

# Main Session Body — Road to West Town

This becomes the core of the session.

## Why This Works Better

Without Bob:

- More RP space for Jack and Midori.
- Easier pacing.
- Stronger bonding opportunity.
- Cleaner spotlight balance.

---

# Road RP Opportunities

## Tray as Conversation Catalyst

Tray can intentionally provoke discussion.

Questions like:

- “So what’s your deal?”
- “You always this quiet?”
- “You trust people easy?”
- “Ever seen someone mutate before?”

This lets:

- Midori reveal pieces naturally.
- Jack react instead of monologuing.

---

# Midway Discovery

Before reaching the farm:

They encounter signs:

- dead birds
- unnatural plant growth
- claw marks on trees
- abandoned supply cart

Optional:  
One mutated animal encounter.

Not a huge fight.  
Just enough to establish:

- corruption is spreading outward

---

# The Farm

## Tone

Silent.  
Wind-heavy.  
No music vibe.

### Visuals

- Doors swinging open
- Livestock dead or missing
- Half-eaten meals
- Black residue in water barrels
- Strange fungal-like Lunafang growths

---

# Key Twist

The people didn’t simply vanish.

They barricaded themselves underground/in the barn.

Why?

Because one family member mutated.

---

# Emotional Encounter

This works especially well for Midori.

A terrified survivor explains:

- Someone sold “medicine”
- Said it would help sickness/fatigue
- Then livestock changed first
- Then people

This shifts Lunafang from:  
“drug gangs”  
to  
“systemic horror affecting normal people.”

---

# Jack’s Investigation Opportunity

Jack notices details:

- Supply crates
- Shipment symbols
- Smuggling marks
- Someone intentionally distributed this here

Potential clue:  
A symbol tied to:

- West Blue smugglers
- or a local merchant company

---

# Final Encounter Options

## Option A — Mutated Family Member

Emotional horror fight.

Pros:

- personal tragedy
- difficult moral tone

---

## Option B — Escaped Experiment

Creature escaped into fields.

Pros:

- more action-heavy
- cleaner combat

---

## Option C — Human Smuggler Cleanup Crew

Someone arrives trying to erase evidence.

Pros:

- conspiracy escalation
- introduces antagonist faction early

This is probably the strongest option for THIS party composition because:

- Jack gets investigation payoff
- Midori gets combat/social tension
- Creates recurring enemies

---

# Ending Cliffhanger Ideas

## Best One

After dealing with the farm:

Jack finds shipping manifests.

Destination:

- Not West Blue.

The shipments are arriving from somewhere much closer.

Then:  
A final symbol/stamp reveals:  
someone on THIS island is manufacturing Lunafang.

---

# Character Development Goals

## Midori

By session end:

- feels partially accepted
- sees stakes beyond himself
- begins integrating into crew dynamics

## Jack

By session end:

- becomes invested because conspiracy affects everyone
- gains investigative momentum
- starts realizing this goes deeper than gang conflict

---

# Overall Session Tone

Think:

- rural horror
- conspiracy mystery
- uneasy partnership
- slow-burn tension instead of nonstop combat

This setup honestly may flow better than the original because it keeps the narrative concentrated instead of fragmented.
