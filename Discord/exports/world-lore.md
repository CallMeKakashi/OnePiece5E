There, Riff ran wild with Cadence—music echoing through broken streets, joy carved out between hunger and fear. But the laughter never drowned out what they saw: beatings in alleys, children punished for noise, lives crushed for existing too loudly. Sound, he realized, was rebellion.

The rebellion base was colder, darker—but honest.
Riff played for the wounded, for the angry, for those who had forgotten how it felt to feel. Cadence stood beside him, steady and burning, their rhythm in sync even when words failed.

Cadence strapped her gear on in silence, Keera waiting at the tunnel mouth. Riff tried to joke, tried to play something light—but the notes wouldn’t come out right. Cadence squeezed his shoulder once before turning away.
