# Driftroot — Scene Sequence (playable)

## Setup / Framing

- Current split: Fenris left alone; Shako and Kalla remain with Dravos watching the festival from afar. Goru has gone to speak with his father.
    
- West docks: **Malak** and **Facade** land in a rowboat. They meet a young woman watering plants outside her house.
    
- South side / old city: injured bandit arrives carrying wounded from prior encounter.
    
- A marine cadet and the marine captain are present in South City. Goru is seen talking with Laziel.
    
- Laziel slips away, Goru later found knocked out in a tent with a strap removed and the strap tossed aside (something taken from him).
    
- Laziel escapes toward the forest; the party follows and encounters desperate homeless men and a small scene where two men drink the serum.
    
- Laziel reveals he stole and reworked the mist device (name below) and summons mist warriors while talking about his purge and Nyx.
    

---

## Named item (machine)

**Mistframe** — a cold iron contraption of gears and glass canisters that spins fragments of condensed "mist-cores" into ephemeral soldiers. Originally a Velencruz prototype; Laziel retooled it to summon mist warriors and feed on whatever "fragments" (dice roll mechanic below) are present.

## Scene 1 — Landing & the Young Woman (Western Docks)

**Hook:** The woman (call her **Ilyra**) is watering a small herb garden. She pauses when Malak and Facade approach.

**Sana’s lines (friendly, cautious):**

> “You two look like island folk, or trouble from the sea. If you’re looking for someone — Shako? Kalla? They were here earlier. Festival’s been strange today.”

**Skill options / checks**

- **Persuasion DC 12** to get reliable info on Shako/Kalla’s last sightings (+2 if you offer help or trade).
    
- **Investigation DC 13** or **Perception DC 12** to notice fresh footprints heading south toward the old city (clues: drag marks, scuffed strap bits).
    
- If they snoop around the house: **Dex (Sleight) DC 13** to glimpse a faded marine insignia token tucked under a pot (links to the cadet).
    

If they lie or look suspicious, Ilyra warns them and asks them to leave — which could cause the bandit to arrive sooner.

---

## Scene 2 — Bandit with Wounded (approach)

A panic-struck bandit (name **Flint**), carrying injured back from the old city encounter, spots them. He approaches belligerent but tired.

**Interaction beats**

- **Flint** sizes them up: “Who are you? If you’re looking to stir trouble, you’ll find it somewhere else.”
    
- If Malak convinces Flint they aren’t a threat (Persuasion DC 13), Flint says he’ll guide them to South City but warns they should leave if they’re “not with the Captain.”
    
- If Flint distrusts them, he tries to shoo them off the island (hostility checks).
    

**Consequences**

- Flint can be an ally to guide them south (useful later to find the tent). If turned away, the party must find the tent themselves (Perception/Survival DC 14).
    

---

## Scene 3 — South City / The Tent

The party arrives: they see a cadet escorted away by two marines (ordered by the captain). Laziel has just left the tent. If they rush in immediately:

They find **Goru knocked out** on a makeshift bed. A strap (leather harness) lies ripped/removed on the ground — whatever Goru carried was taken. The strap looks like it was carrying a small, heavy item (now missing).

**Checks**

- **Medicine DC 12** to stabilize Goru and learn he was hit from behind and dragged briefly.
    
- **Perception DC 13** to notice the strap was cut cleanly (small blade) and faint traces of oily residue (hinting to the Nyxshroud Engine).
    
- **Sleight of Hand DC 14** to recover a hidden scrap from Goru’s person (maybe a fragment/cog used with the machine or a clue).
    

If they exit the tent in time, they see Laziel looking back and smirking as he tells the marine captain to take the cadet away. The captain obeys — their hands are tied by orders or politics.

**Captain’s behavior:** He’s formal, conflicted — obeying an order to detain the cadet. If players try to intervene:

- **Intimidation or Persuasion DC 15** to convince the captain to let the cadet go or reveal why he’s detaining him. If the party pressures, the captain withdraws, stating he “cannot defy orders,” forcing a chase.

## Scene 4 — The Chase to the Forest

If the party gives pursuit, they can follow Laziel (he moves on foot toward the forest and the party may catch sight of him with the Nyxshroud Engine strapped to a pack). Along the way they encounter two homeless men who become a scene:

**The two men**

- Weak, desperate, and fanatical. One holds a blade and shouts, “Stop bothering the gentleman — he promised us a new world!”
    
- Another man (Joe) pulls a waterskin and **uses the serum** on himself and on his companion.
    

**If the party intervenes before serum use:**

- **Athletics DC 14** to disarm a blade, or **Insight DC 13** to see the men are being manipulated.
    

**If party fails to stop the serum:**

- The two men move with unnatural speed/ferocity and rush ahead to confront Laziel’s squad / act as living bait — they will aggressively engage the party and can reveal Laziel’s position.
    

**Optional roleplaying hook:** If the party talks to the men (Persuasion DC 15 or Insight), they might learn the Doctor (the one Laziel referenced) or the promise the men mention — a hook to reveal more of Laziel’s conspiracy.

## Scene 5 — Ambush / The Nyxshroud Engine Activated

Laziel reaches a clearing and unpacks the device — the **Nyxshroud Engine**. He has altered it from Goru’s original designs. He taunts the party:

**Laziel’s speech (use as-is / tweak for tone):**

> “It seems like Nyx was a failure as well... Why did both my sons have to be this weak? No matter. I will see this task fulfilled. I do not know who you are or why you meddle, but you will not stop the purge.”

As he speaks, he feeds **mist-fragments** into the engine and rolls (see mechanic below). The machine vomits forth _Mist Warriors_ — ephemeral soldiers formed of smoke and shadow.

**Combat setup**

- Laziel stands behind/near the engine. Mist Warriors appear around the party (surround, flank).
    
- The mist keeps the battlefield obscured (partial concealment — impose disadvantage on Perception checks not involving hearing).

## Mechanics & Quick Stat Blocks

### Mistframe (mechanic)

- When activated, the GM rolls a **Fragments Die**: 1d4 number of Mist Warriors summoned.
    
- Each time the engine is active, roll a **Corruption Check (1d6)**:
    
    - 1–2: Engine summons **weak mistlings**.
        
    - 3–4: Standard **Mist Warriors** (normal).
        
    - 5–6: **Mist Champions** (stronger, one appears in place of a Warrior).
        
- The engine can be **shut down by**:
    
    - **Disabling/Dex check DC 15** (sabotage gear), or
        
    - **Strength DC 16** to rip its core out (risky), or
        
    - **Targeted attack**: AC 14, HP 30; destroying it ends further summoning (but not the existing mist warriors).
        

### Mist Warrior (minion / swarm-type)

- HP: 18 (6d6), AC: 13, Speed 30 ft.
    
- Attack: **Shadow Blade** +5 to hit, 1d8+3 slashing + 1d4 necrotic.
    
- Special: **Ethereal Grasp.** On hit, target must succeed on Con save DC 13 or have disadvantage on the next attack (as the mist clings).
    
- Resistances: Nonmagical bludgeoning/slashing/piercing half.
    
- Vulnerability: Radiant damage +50% (radiant burns the mist).
    
- If reduced to 0 HP, they dissolve into fog (no corpse).
    

### Mist Champion (elite)

- HP: 36 (8d8), AC: 15, Attack +6, 2d8+4 + 1d6 necrotic.
    
- Special: **Fogwall.** Once per combat creates a 10-ft sphere of heavy obscurement.
    
- Radiant vulnerability and same Ethereal Grasp effect (DC 14).
    

### Laziel — Velencruz Bloodline Swordsman (boss)

- HP: 85 (10d10+30), AC 17 (light armor + finesse).
    
- Attack: +8 to hit, 1d10+5 slashing (longsword) + special: **Velencruz Flourish** — target must save Dex DC 15 or be knocked prone.
    
- Movement: 30 ft.
    
- Special:
    
    - **Calculated Purge.** Twice per combat, Laziel may force an adjacent mist warrior to explode into corrosive mist dealing 2d6 necrotic to adjacent enemies.
        
    - **Tactical Mind.** On initiative 20, he uses **Command** to reposition one Mist Warrior (bonus action).
        
- Resistances: Psychic half; Immune to being frightened.
    
- Weakness: Emotionally brittle — if confronted with evidence that Nyx’s attacker was _not_ an immigrant (or shown mercy evidence), he can be shaken (Wisdom save DC 15 to resist), losing a turn.
    

**Tactics:** Laziel fights expertly, using terrain and the engine. He prefers to keep the engine running — he will not risk being cornered near it, but will use it as long as possible. If the engine is destroyed, he becomes enraged and reckless.

## Dramatic Reveal & Roleplay Beats

During combat or immediately after, Laziel reveals his motive in fragments. Use these beats (drop them as taunts or monologues):

- Hikari (his wife) was stabbed and robbed coming back from the orphanage in Old City. Laziel mourned and became hate-filled.
    
- He burned the Old City to “cleanse” it; Vice Admiral **Horus** (the admiral you referenced) put out the flames and thwarted him.
    
- Five years of planning turned to this purge; he believes the island is poisoned by immigrants and criminals, and the nobles won’t act.
    
- He claims the “Doctor” (the same doctor the party has encountered) bred these “worms” and the city’s rot cannot be cured without purge — he sees himself as the cure.
    
- He took the Nyxshroud Engine from Goru (it was originally Goru’s prototype) and retooled it to summon the mist and manipulate the weak (serum addicts, desperate).
    

**Key moment:** If the party points out inconsistencies (e.g., the assailant that stabbed Nyx was from a noble’s retinue or a marine), or if they show the actual evidence (a medallion or testimony), make Laziel roleplay a crack — grant a Wisdom save or he collapses into grief and confusion, opening a chance to capture him alive.

---

## Alternate non-lethal resolutions

1. **Expose the truth:** If party gathers proof that Nyx’s killer wasn’t an immigrant (investigation, social checks, questioning survivors), they can break Laziel’s conviction — force Wisdom save DC 15, then he surrenders or flees.
    
2. **Disable the Engine:** Focus on the Nyxshroud Engine, destroy it quickly — no more reinforcements, Laziel fights alone and can be captured or killed.
    
3. **Save the Serum Users:** If the party stops Joe before the serum becomes fully active, the homeless men are spared and can testify against Laziel later (reduces his numbers).
    
4. **Public exposure:** Bring the captain/marines back, or have Dravos/Shako/Kalla arrive to arrest Laziel (requires convincing the captain to act).
    

---

## Consequences & Hooks after the fight

- **If Laziel is captured alive:** He can be interrogated, revealing the Doctor’s involvement and hinting at larger experiments (link to your existing serum / Doctor plotline). The Nyxshroud Engine may be confiscated (or stolen later).
    
- **If Laziel escapes with engine:** He retreats into the island interior; tracking him leads to a larger base where he plans the final purge — bigger arc boss.
    
- **If the party fails and engine remains active:** The island’s immigrant quarter suffers an attack; escalation with the Navy or Silk Court (political fallout).
    
- **If Goru is rescued and coherent:** He can reveal what Laziel took — perhaps pages of plans or a key that interacts with the engine (plot MacGuffin).

## Quick encounter stats summary (for GM reference)

- Mist Warriors: use in groups of 2–4 for a low-tier skirmish; scale to 6–8 for a midfight.
    
- Laziel: Elite boss, single-target duels or skirmishes with engine support.
    
- Nyxshroud Engine: HP 30, AC 14; destroyable to stop reinforcements.

- Flint staggers in, dumping an injured man at your feet. He bellows, “You two—what are you here for?” (Perception/Charm moment.)

### **Round 1 – The Reveal**

_(Laziel first activates the machine. Mist soldiers begin forming.)_

**Laziel (cold, calm):**

> “You shouldn’t have come here. This island has already chosen its fate.”  
> _(He slots the die into the machine and lets it roll — it lands with a click.)_  
> “I once built this device to preserve the memory of those we lost... now it will help erase the stains that remain.”

**If interrupted:**

> “Ah, so you know Goru. Then you must know he was too weak to finish what I began.”  
> “That boy clings to mercy as if it could bring her back.”

---

### **Round 2 – The Accusation**

_(As the mist warriors engage. Laziel draws his blade, precise and noble.)_

**Laziel (voice rising with anger):**

> “They called it charity when they opened our gates to the filth of the Old City…  
> but when they came for _my wife_, where was the charity then?”

**He parries a blow and snarls:**

> “Nyx believed kindness could heal rot. Now she feeds it, buried beneath the very streets she tried to save.”

**Optional reaction (to Goru’s name mentioned):**

> “Don’t speak that name! He doesn’t know what happened—he was a _child!_”  
> “I buried the truth to spare him… and he repaid me with betrayal.”

---

### **Round 3 – The Justification**

_(Laziel grows desperate, channeling the machine’s power. Mist thickens.)_

**Laziel (grim, resolute):**

> “When the nobles refused to act, I did what was necessary.”  
> “The fire was a mercy — swift, clean. The kind she deserved.”  
> “But that damned Vice Admiral Horus quenched the flames before justice was done.”

**He flicks blood from his blade, eyes cold:**

> “Five years I planned. Five years I watched this island rebuild on ashes… and smiled, knowing I would bury them again.”

---

### **Round 4 – The Descent**

_(The machine glows brighter, mist warriors reform as fast as they fall.)_

**Laziel (deranged calm):**

> “The Veilspinner doesn’t create soldiers… it remembers them. Each one a shadow of those lost in the flames. My flames.”  
> “You can’t kill what’s already dead.”  
> _(He gestures — the mist warriors reform.)_  
> “They remember her screams too.”

**If bloodied or wounded heavily:**

> “You fight like her... she had that same fire. But even she couldn’t survive the poison of this island.”

---

### **Round 5 – The Collapse**

_(His control starts to falter. Mist grows chaotic.)_

**Laziel (ragged, shouting):**

> “You think this is vengeance? No—this is _balance!_”  
> “For every child stolen, every life lost to their greed—this island must pay its debt in full!”

**If party appeals to reason:**

> “Reason? The nobles reasoned while the gutters bled! I _begged_ them to act!”  
> “Now the fire listens to me—and it will not be reasoned with.”

---

### **Round 6 – The Confession**

_(He’s faltering; the machine sparks. Mist warriors flicker.)_

**Laziel (quiet, trembling):**

> “She begged them for aid that night. I saw her hand reach for mine... and then—nothing.”  
> “When I found her, all that remained was her ribbon... soaked in the blood of her kindness.”

**He glares up, grief twisted into fury:**

> “Don’t you see? Driftroot was poisoned the moment it pitied the unworthy!”

---

### **Round 7 – The Fall**

_(The machine overcharges. Mist collapses inward. Laziel staggers, wounded.)_

**Laziel (weakened, whispering):**

> “Nyx… I did it for you.”  
> “For us…”  
> _(He stumbles, blade lowering.)_  
> “Tell him… tell Goru… he was never meant to see this.”

**If defeated non-lethally:**

> “End it… or this island will burn again.”  
> _(He looks to the smoldering Veilspinner as it pulses faintly.)_  
> “The die… was never meant to stop rolling.”

---

### **Post-Fight Epilogue (optional)**

When Laziel falls, the _Veilspinner_ rolls one last time on its own —  
a faint **“6”** — and the mist around them forms Nyx’s silhouette before dissolving completely.

**If Goru reunites later:**  
He finds the broken die clutched in his father’s hand,  
engraved faintly with the word _“Mercy.”_
