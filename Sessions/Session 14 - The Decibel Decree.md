#  The Sound of Silence

## 📝 Recap & Status

- Location: The Shattered Main Gate of the Capital.
    
- [[Baptiste]]: Standing over unconscious guards, exhausted, confused by his own power.
    
- Rest of the party hiding in bushes.
  

## Island

[[Decibella Kingdom]]

## Session Outline

### Scene 1: The Weight of a King

Context: Immediate aftermath of the [[Haki]] blast. The dust is settling.

Read Aloud (Focus on Baptiste):

"The dust from the shattered gate hangs in the air. The three guards aren't just defeated; they are foaming at the mouth, eyes rolled back, collapsed in a heap. You didn't touch them.

You sit on your horse, chest heaving. For a second, the air around you felt... heavy. Like physical pressure. The world turned gray, and your will crushed theirs. Now, the feeling fades, leaving you with a splitting headache."

The Party Arrives: [[Roma| Roma]], [[Malphas| Malphas]], [[B.O.B| B.O.B]], and [[Cade Tigor Cooper | Tigor]] catch up.

  > Give time for roleplay

The Threat: From high atop the city walls, Megaphones begin to flash red. A mechanical, amplified voice booms across the district (The Emperor's automated system):

"ALERT. DECIBEL SPIKE DETECTED AT NORTH GATE. MAGNITUDE: ILLEGAL. DEPLOYING HOUSE TREMOR."

Action: They cannot stay here. They are exposed.

> Bob guides them to the underground cave

---

### Scene 2: The Silent Slums

Context: Moving through the Commoner District to find the hidden cave.

Atmosphere: The streets are terrifyingly quiet. Commoners with jaw-locks press themselves against walls, terrified of the party. They saw the gate break; they know death is coming.

The Obstacle: [[Echo Spy| House Echo Spies]] As the party moves through the alleyways, have the players make a DC 15 Perception Check.

- Success: They notice their shadows don't look right.
    
- The Encounter: Four members of House Echo are tailing them. They wear gray morph-suits and have tuning forks for weapons.
    
- Echo Agent: "They broke the gate... broke the gate... gate... gate..." (They repeat words unnervingly).
    
- Combat/Stealth: The party must silence these spies before they alert the heavy hitters. The spies will try to record their voice on a Dial to "tag" them for the executioners.
    
---

### Scene 3: The War Room of Discord

**Context:** They find the hidden entrance behind a false wall in a ruined house.

**The Hideout:** It’s not just a cave; it’s a **Command Center**.

- **Visuals:** Soundproofing bedrolls line the walls. Chalkboards display patrol routes of the Nobles.
    
- **The People:** Members of **House Sin** (Punk aesthetic, loud instruments) are working side-by-side with **Commoners** (using sign language and written notes). It is a joint operation.

**NPC Encounter: [[Riff Sin]] (Leader of the Cell)**
  
- **Action:** He is arguing with a Commoner elder (via chalkboard).
    
    - _Riff:_ "We can't wait for anymore! Cadence is probably being tortured right this second!"
        
    - _Elder (Chalkboard):_ "Too risky. We need a distraction."

**The Interaction:** Riff turns his weapon on the party, then lowers it when he sees Baptiste.

> **Riff:** "So, you're the 'Gate Breaker.' The streets are vibrating with the news. A man who screams without opening his mouth.
> 
> Welcome to **The Discord**. We aren't just the 'House of Sin.' We are the voice of this city. The Emperor took their voices (points to Commoners), so we scream for them."

**Lore Drop:**

- **The Plan:** They are planning to hijack the Emperor's massive "Broadcast Tower" to transmit a "Frequency of Freedom" that shatters the Jaw-Locks (which are sound-frequency locked).
    
- **Cadence:** Riff confirms [[Cadence| Cadence ]] was here. "She provided us the schematics for the tower. She's a commoner as well. She went to the Upper City to sabotage the backup generator. We haven't heard from her since. We got help from another outsider. But before we were going to launch the infiltration she just ran and said she had something to witness."

---

### Scene 4: The Bass Drop (Boss Battle)

**Context:** The Empire has tracked the party. The hideout is compromised.

**Read Aloud:**

> "The dust on the floor begins to jump. _Thump... Thump..._ A massive voice amplifies from the street above, shaking the cave supports.
> 
> **'LITTLE RATS! YOU BROKE THE KING'S GATE! I CAN HEAR YOUR HEARTBEATS!'**
> 
> The ceiling cracks. A massive figure smashes through the roof, landing in the center of the War Room."

**The Enemy: [[Baron Woofer| Baron Woofer (House Tremor)]]**

- **Description:** 8.5-foot tall, wearing massive **Speaker-Pauldron Armor**. He carries a two-handed hammer shaped like a Tuning Fork.
    
- **Minions:** 4 **[[Mezzo Troop| Mezzo Troopers]]** drop in behind him.

#### ⚔️ Combat Encounter

Goal: Defeat Woofer or buy time for the Rebels to evacuate.

Environmental Hazard:

- Falling Debris: At the start of every round, falling rocks hit random spots (Dex Save DC 12) as Woofer’s bass shakes the cave.    

---

### Scene 5: The Pact

Context: After the battle. The hideout is damaged, but Woofer is defeated/repelled.

Riff’s Proposal: Riff wipes blood from his lip. He looks at the destruction, then at the determined faces of his crew.

Riff: "We can't stay here. But you... you took down a Baron.

Listen, outsiders. We have the numbers, but we lack the firepower to breach the Tower. You need a way off this island. I assume you didn't leave anyone on your ship to keep guard did you?

The Deal: You help us storm the Broadcast Tower and break the Jaw-Locks. In exchange, House Sin will clear a path for you to get your ship back and leave. Do we have an accord?"

### Scene 6: The Throat of the City

Context: To reach the Upper City (where the Tower and Cadence are) without alerting the entire army, Riff suggests a dangerous shortcut.

The Setup: Riff leads the party to a massive, rusted industrial ventilation shaft that runs vertically through the island's core.

Riff: "We call this 'The Channel.' It carries fresh air to the Nobles and pumps their waste heat down to us. It’s the only way up that House Echo doesn't watch."

The Catch: "But be careful. Every hour, the Emperor recorded announcement run. When he speaks, the sound pressure in this tube is enough to knock you out. You have 45 minutes before the next broadcast. Climb fast."

Skill Challenge: The Vertical Climb

- Athletics (Str): Physically climbing the rusted, vibrating ladders.
    
- Perception (Wis): Noticing loose bolts or steam vents.
    
- Tigor's Moment: Tigor refuses to use the ladder. He digs his "Tiger Claws" (brass knuckles) into the metal walls and climbs on all fours.

The Hazard: The Sound Check Halfway up, the warning lights flash amber. The Emperor clears his throat over the speakers.

- Mechanic: Everyone must make a DC 14 Constitution Saving Throw.
    
- Failure: 2d6 Thunder damage and deafened for 10 minutes as the preliminary static blasts through the shaft.
    
- Success: They manage to cover their ears/mouths in time to mitigate the pressure.  

---

### Scene 7: The Gilded District (High Note)

Context: The party emerges from a decorative grate in the Noble District. The contrast is jarring.

Atmosphere (Sensory Overload):

- Visuals: Everything is gold, marble, and white stone.
    
- Sound: It is deafeningly loud.

- Nobles don't walk; they strut.
    
- They don't talk; they shout through gold-plated cone collars.
    
- Violins and Opera music blast from every street corner.
    
- "HAHAHA! LOVELY WEATHER, ISN'T IT, BARON?!" (A noble screams this inches from Malphas's face).

Stealth/Disguise: The party stands out. They look like "quiet people."

- Action: They need to blend in.
    
- Riff: He can lead the group, pretending they are his "silent servants".

The Objective: Riff meets up a House Sin member they talk and Riff knows now that Cadence’s signal to the Royal Opera House, which sits right across from the Broadcast Tower.

---

### Scene 8: The Aria of Agony

Context: They infiltrate the Royal Opera House to find Cadence. She went there to sabotage the backup generator.

> Stealth section

The Location: A massive backstage area filled with props, costumes, and machinery. In the center room, tied to a large brass pipe, is Cadence.

[[Lady Soefra Anthem| Lady Soefra]]: Standing over her is a tall, slender woman wearing a dress made of stained glass and carrying a baton.

- Lady Soefra (Head of House Anthem): She is the Emperor’s Lead Soloist.
    
- The Situation: Soefra is humming a high-pitched note. The glass in the room is vibrating. She isn't interrogating Cadence with words; she is using sound frequency to torture her.

Read Aloud:

> "Lady Soefra taps her baton against Cadence's cheek. 'What is a lowborn doing here? I remember you rat. You're that little brat aren't you.'
> 
> She inhales deep, her chest expanding unnaturally. 'Let me remind you what a real voice feels like.'"

Combat Encounter: Lady Soefra & The Choir

- The Enemy: Lady Soefra (Caster/Bard archetype) + a lot of Tenor Guards (House Anthem elites with sonic pistols).
    
- Mechanic - The High Note: Every round, Soefra sings a specific note.
	- Round 1: Glass Shatter. All potions/vials in player inventories take damage or break if not protected.
	- Round 2: Vertigo. Wis Save or Disadvantage on attacks due to inner ear imbalance.
	- Round 3: Crescendo. Massive AoE Thunder damage.

The Twist (Mid-Fight): Cadence signs : "THE PIPES! BREAK THE BRASS PIPES!"

- Tactics: Breaking the brass pipes releases steam, which disrupts sound waves, giving Soefra Disadvantage on her sonic attacks.

---

### Scene 9: The Key to the Tower

Context: After defeating Soefra.

The Aftermath: Soefra falls, her glass dress shattering. Cadence collapses, coughing up blood.

Cadence: "You... you actually came." (signing)

The Reward: Cadence pulls a small golden vinyl record from a slit in her hand.

Cadence: "This is it. The Override Disc. If we get this to the top of the Broadcast Tower, we can hijack the Emperor’s signal. We can send a frequency that unlocks every Jaw-Lock on the island at once."

"But the Emperor knows we're here now. Look."

The Cliffhanger: Through the shattered windows of the Opera House, they look up at the massive Broadcast Tower. The lights turn from White to Blood Red. The entire building begins to hum. The Emperor has woken up.

---

#### 📝 Rewards

- Soefra’s Baton: A wand that can cast Thunderwave (3 charges/day).
    
- Noble Collars: Can be salvaged for gold (high value).
    
- Roma Finds a "Sound Dial".

### Scene 10: The Silent Five

Context: Immediately after Soefra’s defeat, looking up at the Tower from the shattered Opera House window.

"Red lights bathe the district. High above, on the main balcony of the Broadcast Tower, five silhouettes step out from the smoke. They don't look like normal soldiers. They stand with a terrifying, relaxed confidence.

Riff turns pale, gripping his sonic rifle until his knuckles turn white. 'No... not them. Those are the Soundless. The Emperor’s Personal Guard. If they are out, the Control Room is completely locked down. We can’t hack it. We need physical keys.'"

The Speech: The figure in the center steps forward. He is dressed in a pristine white suit with a gold conductor’s baton. This is Coda. He speaks into a microphone, his voice crisp and bored.

Coda: *"Mic check. One, two. Sigh. I hate waking up to this ruckus. The Emperor has given you weaklings too much freedom to roam around.

Nonetheless, outsiders, listen closely. This is your last warning. Leave, or your voices will be forever silenced."*

The Symbol of Despair: Coda points his baton upward, toward the very peak of the spire. Hanging there is a massive, rusted iron bell, centuries old.

Coda: "See that bell? That is the Old Liberty Bell. A symbol of despair we keep on display. Many rebellions rose to ring it, and all were destroyed. It has never made a sound in three hundred years. If your goal is to free these people, give up. Despair is heavy."

The Opportunity: B.O.B zooms in with his optical sensors.

B.O.B (Private Comms to Baptiste): "Captain. Structural analysis complete. The mounting bracket of that bell is corroded (Rust Factor: 92%). A single targeted frequency strike—specifically my 'Toll the Dead' protocol—would cause catastrophic structural failure. Probability of the bell crushing the hostile targets: 88%."

The Action: Baptiste gives the nod.

- B.O.B: Points his robotic hand. A spectral, necrotic bell tolls in the air. GONG.
    
- The Result: The rusty chain snaps. The massive, multi-ton iron bell plummets straight toward the balcony.

The Counter:

"Gravity takes hold. The bell falls like a meteor, casting a shadow over the five figures. Riff cheers, thinking it’s over.

But Coda doesn't move. He doesn't even look up.

Two of the figures flanking him—a massive man (Bass) and a slender woman (Treble)—step forward. They draw their weapons in a blur. SHING. SHING.

No explosion. No crash. The massive bell splits cleanly into four quarters. The pieces slide harmlessly past the balcony, crashing into the streets below with a ground-shaking thud.

The five figures remain untouched. Coda dusts a speck of rust off his shoulder."

---

### Scene 11: The Game of Keys

Context: The dust settles. The intimidation check was successful.

Read Aloud:

"Coda looks down at you, amused.

'Very well then. You have spirit. Let’s play a game, shall we?'

He holds up a golden keycard. The other four members hold up identical cards.

'We will wait for you inside the Tower. Level 1, Level 2, Level 3... all the way up. If you can beat us and retrieve all five Keycards, you can unlock the Control Room. I suppose you will have won.'

His smile vanishes. The air gets cold.

'But be warned. You are racing the clock. The Emperor is stirring. If he wakes up fully before you reach the top... you won't fight him. You will bow. Just like the rest.'"

The Departure: The five figures turn and walk back into the tower. The heavy blast doors at the base of the tower hiss open, inviting the party inside.

End of Session 7 Wrap-Up:

- The Stakes: A "Boss Rush" Dungeon Crawl.
    
- The Time Limit: "Before the Emperor Wakes."
    
- The Goal: Defeat the Soundless Five, collect the Keycards, override the signal.
