## CONTINUE THE SESSION

Linus moves through the ship quietly, seeking out Roma first.

He checks him over carefully, watching for any signs of strain.

“Hold still… I just need to make sure.”

A pause as he studies him.

“The serum you took back there… it wasn’t stable.”

He glances up briefly.

“Any dizziness? Fatigue? Anything unusual at all?”

He doesn’t wait for long answers—just observes, methodical, moving on to the next when he’s satisfied.

“One by one… I need to check everyone.”

---

Somewhere else—

Goru prepares to leave.

The ship creaks as it pulls away, quiet but deliberate.

Cassian watches from a distance at first… then follows.

Not openly.

Not loudly.

Just enough to make sure Goru makes it out alive.

No words exchanged.

Just intent.

---
Below deck—

Bob and Cade sit in the quiet after everything.

Bob breaks first.

“I… don’t think I’m built for this.”

A pause.

“Every time things go bad… I’m just there.”

“Not helping. Not changing anything.”

He lets out a dry laugh.

“Kind of ironic, huh? All these upgrades… and I still feel useless.”

Silence hangs there, waiting for Cade.

---
On the upper deck—

Zim approaches Roma, more casual than usual.

“So… Zou, huh?”

He leans on the railing beside him.

“What was it like growing up there?”

A beat.

“You don’t really talk about it.”

Not pressing. Just… asking.

---
Later—

Linus finds Jack.

He doesn’t start immediately.

Just stands there for a moment before speaking.

“There’s something we need to address.”

His tone is calm—but heavier than before.

“The time I took from your crew.”

A pause.

“I didn’t ask.”

“I didn’t explain.”

Another pause.

“But I need to know…”

He finally looks directly at him.

“…what that cost you.”

---

“Guys… I’d like to say something to all of you.”

A small pause.

“First—Baptiste… I’m sorry for hitting you.”

“I was mad. I wasn’t thinking clearly.”

He exhales, steadier now.

“I don’t think I would be here if it wasn’t for… Droven…”

He catches himself, lets out a slight breath.

“…and each and every one of you.”

“There was a tradition on our crew. Instead of thanking each other for saving or helping one another…”

“The captain would just say— ‘Just give me a drink and stop whining.’”

A faint, bittersweet smile.

“I don’t really have that either right now…”

“But next time we have some time… let’s have a few drinks together.”

A pause. The tone shifts.

“Now… unfortunately, I can’t come with you, Baptiste.”

“I can’t leave the North Blue without my captain.”

“I made a vow to him… and to myself… that I will find him.”

“So me and Dravos… we’re going to get off at the next island.”

“I tried to say no to him… but he wouldn’t listen.”

Dravos steps in, voice firm but calm.

“It’s a big brother’s job to protect his younger brother.”
