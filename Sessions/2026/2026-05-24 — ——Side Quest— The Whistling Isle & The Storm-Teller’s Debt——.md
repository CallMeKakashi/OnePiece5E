## Prep (before play)

### **Setup**

After the fighting ring fiasco, Shako and Kalla return to the ship bruised, hungry, and low on morale.  
Moments later, the winds around them whip into a frenzy—**a sudden high-pressure burst** pinning the ship in place. If they try to sail, strong lateral gusts will fling them straight into the Calm Belt. They're forced to anchor.

The crew realizes they only have **2 days of rations left**.

A crewmate spots **a small island** obscured by rolling mist in the distance—just barely reachable by rowboat.

---

# **The Island: “Whistling Isle”**

A rocky, forested island surrounded by wind tunnels carved into the cliffs. The entire place _sings_—low moans, flute-like whistles, sudden humming—depending on the direction of the gales.

Locals live in a small settlement called **Galehome**, a village built to withstand constant wind pressure.

Shako and Kalla are sent to gather supplies.

---

# **The Core Problem**

The village is in chaos.

A normally reliable local phenomenon—the **Wind Oracle**, an elderly woman who can “read” the island’s shifting winds—has vanished.  
Without her predictions, the islanders cannot hunt, harvest, or forage safely, because deadly gusts come unpredictably and can blow people off cliffs.

The villagers agree to help Shako and Kalla _only if they find the Oracle_.

---

# **What Actually Happened**

A group of opportunistic scavengers—the **Wind-Takers**, a band of glider-winged thieves—kidnapped the Oracle.  
They want her to reveal where a legendary cache of skyminer crystals lies hidden beneath the island.

They keep her inside a huge hollow **wind tunnel cave** that amplifies sound so much it disorients intruders.

---

# **Quest Beats**

### **1. Arrival & Negotiation**

- Galehome is tense, shutters shut, ropes tied everywhere to protect from sudden gusts.
    
- Villagers refuse to trade.
    
- Kalla could do some sociable roleplay; Shako could be suspicious and scan the environment.
    
- Eventually, they learn: _Oracle missing = no harvest = no supplies._
    

### **2. Searching the “Wind Paths”**

The pair must navigate:

- Narrow ledges
    
- Sudden gust traps
    
- Cliffside wind chimes that act as clues (the tones indicate wind direction changes)
    

Maybe a **skill challenge**:

- Athletics to resist gusts
    
- Acrobatics to move along narrow paths
    
- Perception to listen to tonal clues
    
- Survival to follow tracks
    

### **3. Encounter: The Wind-Takers**

A lightweight, agile crew with:

- Wing gliders
    
- Hooked claws
    
- Gao-like masks that help them “hear” wind shifts
    

They leap between air currents and use wind-pressure slashes.

Can be CR 3–5 depending on group size.

Leader: **Breezer “Gale-Bite” Roci**, who uses compressed air techniques.

### **4. The Oracle’s Cave**

Inside the resonant wind tunnel:

- Sounds echo violently
    
- Speaking loudly causes shockwave feedback
    
- Attacks that create noise (like gunshots, thunder, roars) bounce back as damage
    

Perfect for Kalla to use quiet precision and Shako to be careful.

Oracle is bound but unharmed, focusing on calming the island winds.

---

# **5. Twist: The Oracle Knew They Were Coming**

When rescued, she says something cryptic like:

> “The winds whispered your names days ago.  
> Your fates trail storms behind them.”

She then guides them to an **abandoned skyminer field**, where the village stores emergency rations.

They gain:

- Enough food for the ship
    
- Some bonus exotic herbs
    
- A wind-tuned trinket (maybe a seashell that whistles when danger approaches)
    

---

# **6. Return to the Ship**

As soon as they return with supplies, the winds around the island shift calmly—almost like the Oracle commanded them to let the ship go.

They escape without starving.
